[tex] \Large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTIONS}:} [/tex]

A. Kite ABCD with diagonals BD and AC

The area of the kite is half the product of its two diagonals.

[tex] \begin{align} & \bold{Formula:} \\ & \boxed{\rm Area = \frac{\,d_1 \cdot d_2\,}{2}} \end{align} [/tex]

#1:

» Find the area in which the two diagonals measures 5cm and 12cm.

[tex] Area = \frac{\,\overline{BD} \cdot \overline{AC}\,}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] Area = \frac{\,5\,cm \cdot 12\,cm\,}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] Area = \frac{\,60\,cm^2\,}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] Area = 30\,cm^2 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The area of the kite is 30cm²

[tex] \: [/tex]

#2:

» Find diagonal AC in which the area is 90cm² and the other diagonal measures 6cm.

[tex] Area = \frac{\,\overline{BD} \cdot \overline{AC}\,}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] 90cm^2 = \frac{\,6\,cm \cdot \overline{AC}\,}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] 90cm^2 \cdot 2 = \frac{\,6\,cm \cdot \overline{AC}\,}{2} \cdot 2 \\ [/tex]

[tex] 180cm^2 = 6\,cm \cdot \overline{AC}\\ [/tex]

[tex] \frac{\,180cm^2\,}{6\,cm} = \frac{\,6\,cm \cdot \overline{AC}\,}{6\,cm} \\ [/tex]

[tex] 30cm = \overline{AC} [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] Diagonal AC measures 30cm.

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

B. Trapezoid ABCD with median EF.

The measure of the median is half the sum of the two bases of a trapezoid.

[tex] \begin{align} & \bold{Formula:} \\ & \boxed{\rm Median = \frac{\,b_1 + b_2\,}{2}} \end{align} [/tex]

#1:

» Find x if base DC is x, Median EF is 5, and Base AB is 11.

[tex] \overline{EF} = \frac{\,\overline{DC} + \overline{AB}\,}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] 5 = \frac{\,x + 11\,}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex] 5 \cdot 2 = \frac{\,x + 11\,}{2} \cdot 2 \\ [/tex]

[tex] 10 = x + 11 [/tex]

[tex] x = 10 - 11 [/tex]

[tex] x = \text- 1 \\ [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The value of x is -1.

[tex] \: [/tex]

#2:

» The median of the trapezoid bisects the two legs. Thus, line DE and line EA are congruent. Find the value of x and the measure of EA.

[tex] \overline{DE} \cong \overline{EA} [/tex]

[tex] 8 = x - 4 [/tex]

[tex] x = 8 + 4 [/tex]

[tex] x = 12 [/tex]

» Therefore, x is 12. Find the measure of line EA.

[tex] \overline{EA} = x - 4 [/tex]

[tex] \overline{EA} = 12 - 4 [/tex]

[tex] \overline{EA} = 8 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The value of x is 12 and the measure of line EA is 8 units.

27. • Differentiate quadrilateral from parallelogram. • How would you know if a quadrilateral is a parallelogram? • Explain why every parallelogram is a quadrilateral but not every quadrilateral is a parallelogram. (M9Q3W1 – Quadrilaterals and Parallelograms)​


→ A quadrilateral is a four-sided polygon while a parallelogram is a quadrilateral with two sets of opposite sides that are parallel.

→ A parallelogram is a quadrilateral that:

a. A quadrilateral's diagonals are bisected by each other.

b. The opposite sides of both pairs of opposite sides are congruent.

c. Both opposite angle pairs are congruent.

d. Both pairs of sides are congruent and parallel.

e. Both successive angles are supplementary to a quadrilateral's angle.

→ Because all parallelograms have four sides, they are always quadrilaterals. While a quadrilateral isn't necessarily a parallelogram because it doesn't always have two pairs of opposite sides that are parallel.
