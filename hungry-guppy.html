13. In Sean's fish tank, there are 6 times as many goldfish as guppies. There are a total of 21 fish in the tank. How many more goldfish are there than guppies


Let:
x = guppies
y = goldfish

guppies = goldfish
x = 6y

A group of fish is 7 units, one guppy and six goldfish.

21 ÷ 7 = 3

There are three groups of fish, so: 3*(x + 6y)
21 = 3x + 18y

Three guppies and eighteen goldfish.

18 - 3 = 15
There are 15 more goldfish then guppies.

Answer:

The exposition of the story "The Hungry Mouse" sets up the main characters and introduces the problem. The story typically begins by introducing the main character, a mouse who is hungry and searching for food. The mouse may be described as small, weak, and vulnerable, highlighting the challenges it faces in finding food. 30. 1/5 of the fishes in the aquarium are goldfish 3/4 of the fishes are swordfish and the rest are guppies there are 20 guppies in the aquarium. How many fishes are there in all in the aquarium


Answer:

All in all, there are 400 fishes in the aquarium.

Step-by-step explanation:

1/5 + 3/4 = 4/20 + 15/20 = 19/20

1 - 19/20 = 1/20 (guppies)

20(4) = 80

20(15) = 300

80 + 300 + 20 = 400
