1. what is the classification of suling instrument?​


Answer:

The Maguindanaon suling is the smallest bamboo flute of the Maguindanaon and the only one classified as a ring-flute (the other two bamboo flutes of the Maguindanaon, the tumpong and the palendag are both lip-valley flutes).


2. Suling instrument classification and Origin?​


Answer:

A suling or Seruling is an Indonesian bamboo ring flute. Originating from Sundanese music in West Java, Indonesia. It is popular in many other Southeast Asian countries including Brunei, Malaysia, Philippines, and Singapore.

11. Describe and identify the classificatkon (horn bostel classification) of the following musical instruments.1. Kulintang2. Aggung3. Gabbang4. Kudyapi5. Suling​


Answer:

1.Kulintang is a type of percussion instrument that originates from the Philippines. It is classified under the Hornbostel-Sachs classification as an idiophone, specifically a set of tuned gongs played with mallets. Its classification code is 111.242.

2.Agung or agong is a large gong used in the Philippines as part of traditional ensembles. It is classified under the Hornbostel-Sachs classification as an idiophone, specifically a suspended gong. Its classification code is 111.242.1.

3.Gabbang is a xylophone-like instrument from the southern Philippines, particularly among the Tausug people. It is classified under the Hornbostel-Sachs classification as an idiophone, specifically a wooden keyed instrument. Its classification code is 111.212.

4.Kudyapi is a two-stringed boat-lute from the Philippines. It is classified under the Hornbostel-Sachs classification as a chordophone, specifically a lute. Its classification code is 321.322.

5.Suling is a bamboo flute from Indonesia, particularly among the Sundanese people. It is classified under the Hornbostel-Sachs classification as an aerophone, specifically a side-blown flute. Its classification code is 421.111.
