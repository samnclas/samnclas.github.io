2. A person with a black belt in karate has a fist that has a mass of 0.70 kg. starting from rest, this fist attains a velocity of 8.0 m/s in 0.15 s. what is the magnitude of the average net force applied to the fist to achieve this level of performance?


The average net force applied : 37.33 N

Further explanation  

Newton's 2nd law explains that the acceleration produced by the resultant force on an object is proportional and in line with the resultant force and inversely proportional to the mass of the object  

[tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{a=\dfrac{\sum F}{m}}}[/tex]

Acceleration itself is a change in velocity within a certain time interval

[tex]\rm a=\dfrac{\Delta v }{\Delta t}[/tex]

the two equations above can be made equations:

[tex]\rm \dfrac{\sum F}{m}=\dfrac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}\\\\\sum F=m\dfrac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}[/tex]

A fist that has a mass of 0.70 kg. starting from rest, this fist attains a velocity of 8.0 m/s in 0.15 s., so

m = 0.7 kg

vf = final velocity = 8 m/s

vi = initial velocity = 0 m/s(from rest)

[tex]\rm \Delta v=v_f-v_i\\\\\Delta v=8-0\\\\\Delta v=8~m/s[/tex]

Δt = 0.15 s

The average net force :

[tex]\rm \sum F=0.7\times \dfrac{8}{0.15}\\\\\sum F=\boxed{\bold{37.33~N}}[/tex]

3. A boxer has a fist of 0.6 kg. starting from rest, his fist attains a velocity of 7m/s as the punching bag is hit. how much impulse is imparted on the punching bag?​


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

A boxer has a fist of 0.6 kg. starting from rest, his fist attains a velocity of 7m/s as the punching bag is hit. how much impulse is imparted on the punching bag?​

Note:

Impulse is equal to change in momentum, therefore we can use the formula Impulse = mass × velocity

Given Data:

m ( mass ) = 0.6 kg

v ( velocity ) = 7 m/s

I ( impulse ) = ? unknown

Solution:

I = m × v

  = 0.6 kg × 7 m/s

  = 4.2 kg·m/s

Answer:

The Impulse is equal to 4.2 kg·m/s

6. describe man with a fist of rock​


Answer:

The Thinker

A man sits alone on a rock, absorbed in thought. He leans forward, with his elbow on his knee and hand supporting his chin. Clearly focused, he intently casts his eyes downward. Known as The Thinker, this bronze sculpture represents the creative mind at work. Although the figure is seated, he is not at rest. As Auguste Rodin (ah-GOOST roe-DEN), the French artist who created this sculpture, stated, “What makes my Thinker think is that he thinks not only with his brain, with his knitted brow, his distended nostrils, and compressed lips, but with every muscle of his arms, back, and legs, with his clenched fist and gripping toes.”1 By choosing to depict The Thinker as a strong, athletic figure, Rodin conveyed that the act of thinking is a powerful exercise.

The Thinker was originally conceived as part of Rodin’s design for a set of bronze doors for a museum in Paris. This figure represented Dante Alighieri, an early Italian Renaissance poet. Rodin depicted Dante reflecting on The Divine Comedy, his epic poem about heaven, hell, and the fate of all humankind. However, the sculpture ultimately came to symbolize everyone who utilizes their imagination to create: artists, writers, scientists, and many others. Rodin himself identified with The Thinker, and a version of the sculpture still overlooks his tomb today.

There are several versions of this sculpture in different sizes. This one is just over two feet tall and is the size of Rodin’s handmade clay model, from which many bronze casts were made. Well-known around the world, The Thinker continues to celebrate humankind’s creative accomplishments.

21. Write the fist 5 term of geometric whose fist term is 2 and the common ratio is 3​


Answer:

2, 6, 18, 54, 162

Step-by-step explanation:

We need to multiply the common ratio to the previous term in order to get the next term.  

a₁ = 2                  a₁ × r = a₂         →        2 × 3 = 6a₂ × r = a₃        →         6 × 3 = 18a₃ × r = a₄        →        18 × 3 = 54a₄ × r = a₅        →        54 × 3 = 162

Therefore the first five terms of the geometric sequence is 2, 6, 18, 54, and 162.
