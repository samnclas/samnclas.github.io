17. Instruction: Find the critical value in each of the given information.1. One-tailed test (right-tailed), variance is known, a = 0.05, n = 342. Two-tailed test, variance is known, a = 0.05, n = 453. Two-tailed test, variance is unknown, a = 0.01, n = 94. One-tailed tesi (left-tailed), variance is unknown, a = 0.01, n = 165.Two-tailed test variance is unknown, n = 0.05 n = 10​


Answer:

Reject H0 if T < 73.361 or T > 128.422

Step-by-step explanation:

A chi-square test ( Snedecor and Cochran, 1983) can be used to test if the variance of a population is equal to a specified value. This test can be either a two-sided test or a one-sided test. The two-sided version tests against the alternative that the true variance is either less than or greater than the specified value. The one-sided version only tests in one direction. The choice of a two-sided or one-sided test is determined by the problem. For example, if we are testing a new process, we may only be concerned if its variability is greater than the variability of the current process.

Definition The chi-square hypothesis test is defined as:

H0: σ2=σ20

Ha:  

σ2<σ20     for a lower one-tailed test

σ2>σ20     for an upper one-tailed test

σ2≠σ20     for a two-tailed test

Test Statistic: T=(N−1)(s/σ0)2

where N is the sample size and s is the sample standard deviation. The key element of this formula is the ratio s/σ0 which compares the ratio of the sample standard deviation to the target standard deviation. The more this ratio deviates from 1, the more likely we are to reject the null hypothesis.

Significance Level: α.

Critical Region: Reject the null hypothesis that the variance is a specified value, σ02, if

T>χ21−α,N−1     for an upper one-tailed alternative

T<χ2α,N−1     for a lower one-tailed alternative

T<χ2α/2,N−1

   or

T>χ21−α/2,N−1     for a two-tailed alternative

where χ2.,N−1 is the critical value of the chi-square distribution with N - 1 degrees of freedom.

The formula for the hypothesis test can easily be converted to form an interval estimate for the variance:

(N−1)s2χ21−α/2,N−1−−−−−−−−−⎷≤σ≤(N−1)s2χ2α/2,N−1−−−−−−−−−⎷

A confidence interval for the standard deviation is computed by taking the square root of the upper and lower limits of the confidence interval for the variance.

Chi-Square Test Example A chi-square test was performed for the GEAR.DAT data set. The observed variance for the 100 measurements of gear diameter is 0.00003969 (the standard deviation is 0.0063). We will test the null hypothesis that the true variance is equal to 0.01.

H0:  σ2 = 0.01

Ha:  σ2 ≠ 0.01

Test statistic:  T = 0.3903

Degrees of freedom:  N - 1 = 99

Significance level:  α = 0.05

Critical values:  Χ 2α/2,N-1 = 73.361  

                 Χ 21-α/2,N-1 = 128.422

Critical region: Reject H0 if T < 73.361 or T > 128.422

27. What criteria are used to upgrade a tropical storm to a typhoon? a. Eye of the typhoon b. Direction c. Tail of the typhoon d. Wind speed​


WHAT CRITERIA ARE USED TO UPGRADE A TROPICAL STORM TO A TYPHOON?

The answer would be letter "D", wind speeds. Depending on where the storm begins in the world, it is categorized as a hurricane, typhoon, or tropical cyclone after it reaches wind speeds of 74 miles per hour or greater.

A pre-existing tropical disturbance, warm tropical waters, moisture, and relatively mild winds are all elements for tropical cyclones. We link this phenomenon with high winds, big waves, torrential rains, and floods when the correct conditions are present for a long time. In the Atlantic basin and the central and eastern North Pacific basins, a weather system that does not match all of these criteria but is expected to deliver tropical storm or hurricane-force winds to land in the next day or two is referred to as a potential tropical cyclone.

29. 20. In which part of the typhoon is the wind speed fastest and most violent? a. eye c. eyewallb. tail d. main body​


Answer:

c. eyewall

Explanation:

he most dangerous and destructive part of a tropical cyclone is the eyewall. Here winds are strongest, rainfall is heaviest, and deep convective clouds rise from close to Earth's surface to a height of 15,000 metres (49,000 feet).

The eye is a region of mostly calm weather at the center of tropical cyclones. The eye of a storm is a roughly circular area, typically 30–65 kilometers (19–40 miles) in diameter. It is surrounded by the eyewall, a ring of towering thunderstorms where the most severe weather and highest winds occur.
