17. What is the recursive formula of the sequence 8, 14, 26, 50, ___, ___.


Answer:

98+96=194

Step-by-step explanation:

Answer:

8, 14, 26, 50, 98, 192.

Step-by-step explanation:

As you can see the pattern od the sequence is not that difficult.

The difference between 8 to 14 is 6, 14 to 26 is 12, 26 to 50 is 24.

then, its understandable that the difference between the sequence is being multiplied by 2.

6×2=12, 12×2=24.

so, the next diffence is you need to multiply 24 by 2 to get the next sequence which is;

24×2=48

add 78 to the last number of the sequence which is 50, then you'll get;

48+50= 98 (98 is the next sequence)

Then, 48×2=96 so, the difference between 98 and the last sequence is 96.

98+96=192

the sequence are 8, 14, 26, 50, 98, 192

Explanation:

Definition of Volume

Volume is defined as the amount of space taken up by an object.  It is measured in "cubic" units. The volume of a figure is the number of cubes required to fill it completely, like blocks in a box.

Formulas

We have different volume formulas for each figure. The formulas are as follows:

Cube - volume is equal to [tex]s^{3}[/tex] where s is the side of the cube, [tex]V=S^{3}[/tex].Rectangular Prism - volume is equal to L times W times H,  where L is the length, W is the width and H is the height, [tex]V=LxWxH[/tex].Cylinder - volume is equal to [tex]\pi[/tex][tex]r^{2}[/tex][tex]h[/tex], where r is the radius and h is the height, [tex]V=\pi r^{2} h[/tex].Sphere - volume is equal to [tex]\frac{4}{3}[/tex][tex]\pi[/tex][tex]r^{3}[/tex] where r is the radius.Pyramid - volume is equal to [tex]\frac{1}{3}Ah[/tex], where A is the area of the base and h is the height, [tex]V=\frac{1}{3} Ah[/tex].Cone - volume is [tex]\frac{1}{3} \pi r^{2} h[/tex], where r is the radius and h is the height.

Be sure to use the same units for all measurements. You cannot multiply feet times inches times yards or meters times centimeters to meters. it doesn't make a perfectly cubed measurement. For those units, you need to make conversions before proceeding.
