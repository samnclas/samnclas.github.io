2. give 3 investment problem with answer and solution (create)​


Step-by-step explanation:

Problem 1:

You have $10,000 that you want to invest for 5 years. You have found an investment that pays an annual interest rate of 6%, compounded annually. How much will your investment be worth after 5 years?

Solution:

First, we need to find the future value (FV) of the investment using the formula:

FV = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where:

P = the principal amount (in this case, $10,000)

r = the annual interest rate (6%)

n = the number of times the interest is compounded in a year (1)

t = the number of years (5)

Plugging in the numbers, we get:

FV = $10,000(1 + 0.06/1)^(1*5)

FV = $10,000(1.3382)

FV = $13,382

Therefore, your investment will be worth $13,382 after 5 years.

Problem 2:

You want to invest $5,000 in a mutual fund that pays an annual interest rate of 8%, compounded monthly. If you plan to leave your money in the account for 10 years, how much will you have at the end of the period?

Solution:

To solve this problem, we use the formula:

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where:

P = the principal amount (in this case, $5,000)

r = the annual interest rate (8%)

n = the number of times the interest is compounded in a year (12)

t = the number of years (10)

Plugging in the numbers, we get:

A = $5,000(1 + 0.08/12)^(12*10)

A = $5,000(1.0833)^120

A = $13,600.97

Therefore, you will have $13,600.97 at the end of the 10-year period.

Problem 3:

You want to invest $2,000 in a stock that pays an annual dividend of $1.50 per share. If the stock is currently trading at $30 per share, how many shares should you buy?

Solution:

To solve this problem, we need to find out the dividend yield of the stock using the formula:

Dividend yield = Annual dividend / Current stock price

In this case, the dividend yield is:

Dividend yield = $1.50 / $30

Dividend yield = 0.05 or 5%

This means that the stock pays a dividend of 5% per year. To find out how many shares you should buy, we use the formula:

Shares = Investment amount / Stock price

Plugging in the numbers, we get:

Shares = $2,000 / $30

Shares = 66.67

Therefore, you should buy 66 shares of the stock
