…See moreThe one million dollar question: What is Total Connected Load and Maximum Demand?Total Connected Load (TCL) is the mechanical and electrical load (in kW) that will be connected (or to consumed) for that particular area.The Maximum Demand (MD) is the total kW that actually contributes the total power used in one time after applying the diversity factor based on the Total Connected Load calculated.Example 1Let say, your own bedroom is having 1no. 2hp air-conditioning split unit, 4nos. of single phase socket outlet and 1no. 2X36W T8 Florescent Tube.I’m using the Excel program since I can easily manipulate all the values in case fine-tuning values need to be made in future.Then, your Total Connected Load and the Maximum Demand will be as per above; md.jpg

By looking into the figure above, the Total Connected Load and the Maximum Demand is 2.57kW and 1.36kW respectively.

How The Calculation Works?1. The unit for TCL and MD is in Watt. Therefore, all the loads need to convert into Watt.a) 2hp a/cond split unit = 2 X 746W = 1492Wb) 2X36W Flou. Fitting = (2 X 36W) + Ballast Wattage = 80Wc) 13A Socket Outlet = 250W (this is rather subjective since some engineer putting 300W/nos)2. Determine the Diversity Factor (DF) for the respective load. The DF is the percentage of load that will contribute for the total of the Maximum Demand. For the above example, I’m expecting the split unit will contribute 6o% of the total MD, lighting will be 80% and socket outlet is 40%.3. Multiply the Diversity Factor with the Connected Load to get the Maximum Demand.4. Summing-up the individual MD to obtain the Total MD..Why I Need To Calculate The TCL and MD?By calculating the TCL, you’ll know the total load connected for a particular area and also you can determine the sizing of cables. But, the most important thing is by having the TCL, you can determine your MD. This MD will be declared to the utility provider for the purpose of meter deposit and utility bill.

4. How important Ferdinand E. Marcos Jr is/are his plans for nat'building in the Philippines. Essay​


Answer:

As a pivotal figure in Philippine history, Ferdinand E. Marcos Jr’s plans for nation-building have played a significant role in shaping the country’s economy and political landscape. Marcos Jr’s vision for the Philippines was primarily focused on modernization, and stabilization of the nation in the global scene. In this essay, I will discuss the importance of Ferdinand E. Marcos Jr, and how his plans for nation-building have impacted the Philippines.

Marcos Jr served as the longest-serving president of the Philippines, from 1965 to 1986. During his presidency, he implemented various policies aimed at creating an industrialized and modernized Philippines. One of these policies was the ‘New Society’ policy, a development plan aimed at reducing poverty and increasing economic growth. The policy involved the creation of several bodies to oversee the implementation of developmental projects, such as the National Economic and Development Authority (NEDA), and the National Industrialization Council (NIC). These institutions aimed to coordinate and prioritize industrialization projects and ultimately drive economic development.

One of the significant achievements of Marcos Jr’s nation-building plan was the massive infrastructure development that took place in the country. As part of his vision for modernization, Marcos Jr intensified public investment in infrastructure, constructing roads, bridges, ports, and airports. These developments helped to connect remote areas with urban centers, facilitating commerce and economic growth across the country. Similarly, Marcos Jr’s administration focused on expanding education and healthcare, creating more opportunities for Filipinos to access essential services.

Another critical aspect of Marcos Jr’s nation-building plan was his foreign policy initiatives. His administration sought to strengthen ties with western countries and foster regional cooperation to promote trade and commerce. For instance, during his tenure, Marcos Jr actively courted foreign investors to the Philippines, helping to boost the country’s foreign direct investment, and improve economic growth rates.

Although Marcos Jr’s vision for nation-building was focused on modernization, his methods have often come under scrutiny, primarily due to the perceived authoritarian style of governance during his tenure. Critics point to widespread human rights violations, corruption, and an impervious regime that often disregarded the rule of law. The effects of his rule are still felt today.

In conclusion, despite the controversies that marred Ferdinand E. Marcos Jr’s administration, his plans for nation-building have had a profound impact on the Philippines. His vision for modernization and infrastructure development helped to create an environment that fostered economic growth, and significantly improved the quality of life for many Filipinos. However, he remains a highly polarizing figure in Philippine history, and his legacy often serves as a cautionary tale for the abuse of power. Nonetheless, the impact of his nation-building plan on the Philippine economy and infrastructure cannot be understated, and it remains a significant part of the country’s political and economic history.

Answer:

Rodriguez saw himself as not a good reader or bookish despite having read many authors in high school because he realized that his academic achievement in reading and writing did not necessarily translate to his social and cultural identity as a Mexican-American student. He felt a cultural divide between his home life and the education he received in school. His family background emphasized practical knowledge and skills, while his school education emphasized abstract thinking and academic achievements.

Rodriguez's self-assessment can be seen as both harsh and honest. On the one hand, he had read numerous authors and was quite well-read for his age, which suggests he had some skills in reading and comprehension. On the other hand, he recognized that his reading was not grounded in a broader appreciation for literature, but rather a means of achieving academic success. By acknowledging his shortcomings, Rodriguez was able to critically examine his own identity and experiences, which ultimately led to his personal growth and success

24. How you can contribute Ferdinand E. Marcos Jr. to his plans or Agendas? ESSAY.​


Answer:

Ferdinand E. Marcos Jr, also known as Bongbong Marcos, is a prominent politician and statesman in the Philippines. He is the son of the late dictator Ferdinand E. Marcos, who ruled the country for more than two decades. Bongbong has been actively involved in the country's politics, serving in both national and local government positions. He has proposed various plans for nation-building in the Philippines throughout his career.

One of Bongbong's most well-known plans for nation-building is his "Marcos Vision 21," a comprehensive plan which aims to address the country's most pressing issues. It includes a focus on improving infrastructure, education, agriculture, and tourism. The plan aims to make the country more globally competitive and to create more jobs for Filipinos.

Another one of his proposals is to establish a "Department of Migration and Development," which would address the issue of overseas Filipino workers (OFWs) and their socio-economic impact on the country. This department would ensure that OFWs are properly trained and equipped with the necessary skills to contribute effectively to the country's development.

While Bongbong Marcos' plans for nation-building may have potential benefits, they also come with controversies. His family's history of dictatorial rule and alleged human rights violations have caused some Filipinos to view him with skepticism. Others have criticized his plans for not being comprehensive enough and for lacking concrete details.

In conclusion, Bongbong Marcos' plans for nation-building in the Philippines can have a significant impact on the country's development. However, they also raise questions about the Marcos family's past actions and intentions. It is up to the Filipino people to decide whether or not they support his proposals and to hold their leaders accountable for their actions.
