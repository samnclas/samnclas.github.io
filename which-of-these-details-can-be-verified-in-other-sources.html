1. Which of these details can be verified in other sources? These details can be verified in other sources by checking to see if the facts presented in the article appear in other articles written about the same topic. This can be done by using an online search engine, looking at other websites, or talking to experts in the field to confirm the accuracy of the information.

2. Could these details be true? This question cannot be answered without further verification. It is possible that some or all of the details are true, or that some or all of the details could be false.

3. Which of these facts are measurable? Measurable facts are facts that can be quantified and easily compared to other sources. Examples of measurable facts may include exact dates of events, numbers, and statistics.

4. Is the article written with care? Careful writing is an indication that the writer has put in effort to ensure the accuracy of the information provided. Signs of careful writing include diligent fact-checking, the use of reliable sources, and an attention to detail.

5. Who would find this article valuable? The value of an article will depend on its content and the audience. Some readers may find the information useful for research purposes, while others may view it as entertainment. Ultimately, it is up to the reader to decide which articles are valuable to them.

6. Who wrote the article? It is important to check the author of an article to make sure the information provided is accurate and unbiased. The author should be credited at the beginning or end of the article.

7. Are the sources properly cited? Citing sources is essential for both the credibility of the article and for the reader’s understanding. It is important to include a list of sources at the end of the article, as well as in-text citations, so that other readers may validate the information provided.

8. When was the article written? The date of an article is important to ensure that the information is up-to-date. It is important to include the date of publication or last update in the article to allow other readers to know when the information was provided.
