1. Type of badminton shots that are perfect to variate the pace of the rally


In playing badminton, mastery of basic techniques is required, one of which is the technique of hitting the shuttlecock. Various types of shot techniques in badminton sports games, including:

1. Lob Shot

Lob is an important hit, both for the pattern of defense (defensive) and in the pattern of attack (offensive). This blow can be done from under the head (under head lob) or from above the head (over head lob). This blow is divided into two types, namely:

- Attack Lob

As the name suggests, lob hits are useful for attacking. The hallmark of the attack lob is that the blow makes the shuttlecock go faster and the direction of the bounce is rather low. There are two ways to hit an attack lob, namely an attack lob from above and an attack lob from below.

- Parry Lob

A parry lob is aimed at defending an attack or parrying an attack. The hallmark of this lob is the shuttlecock shot that is bounced high and far to the back of the opponent's court.

2. Service Shot

Serve is a badminton sport technique to hit the shuttlecock to the front, back, right, or left when starting the match and add points. There are two types of services that need to be known, namely:

- Forehand Service

Badminton forehand strokes are divided into two types, namely short and high forehands. The short forehand is done by swinging the racket and is not too strong. Meanwhile, high forehand requires strong power.

- Backhand Service

Backhand service is done by swinging the racket using moderate force. If the right hand holds the racket, then the right foot must be in front, and vice versa.

3. Drive Shot

Drive shot is a flat stroke with moderate speed, and a height above the head, parallel to or slightly below the head with the aim of dropping the shuttlecock on the court.

The benefit of the drive stroke is to speed up the tempo of the game by launching the shuttlecock as low as possible and level with the net. In addition, the drive punch also serves to block the opponent's position.

4. Drop Shot

Drop Shot is a shot made with the aim of making the shuttlecock fall close to the opponent's net.

There are two types of dropshots commonly known in badminton, namely slow dropshot and fast dropshot.

5. Flick Shot

Flick Shot is a shot that is done like a short serve, but when almost touching the shuttle, suddenly the hand is thrown so that the speed of the shuttlecock becomes fast and soars behind the service area. This type of serve is widely used in doubles games.

6. Smash Shot

This type of smash hit activates the wrist to be whipped later at the same time pressed to the bottom. Later, the shuttlecock speed is not so fast but very steep and deep. This makes the opponent helpless.

Types of smash hits:

- Smash lashes

- Circular smash

- Full smash

- Smash slices

4. 3 advantages when you do badminton smash shot​?


Answer:

Smashes encourage a block return to the net, which — if you are quick to move in — creates a chance for playing tight spinning net shots.

It’s important to understand that this benefit does not exist unless you are very fast to move in after your opponent’s block. If you are slow to move forwards, then your opponent will gain the advantage instead.

Other returns of smash can also be used, of course: your opponent could play a lift, drive, or long block (push). Yet these returns also offer you attacking chances.

A long block is a risky shot with very little margin for error, and for that reason is rarely used. If your opponent plays it perfectly, then you should probably lift the shuttle (although you might consider entering a drive war instead). If his long block is too high, however, it offers you an easy opportunity to win the rally with a smash from the front midcourt (or at least a steeply downwards drive).
