24. características de la chanson​


CHARACTERISTICS OF THE CHANSON

CHANSONChanson means "song" in French. Chanson music encompasses many periods of French song, ranging from monophonic chant in the Middle Ages through polyphonic singing in the Renaissance. Modern chanson music bridges the gap between nineteenth-century nightclub music in Paris and modern pop music.

CHARACTERISTICS

Every chanson is monophonic. Ars nova composers created both holy and profane songs. Music has generally been unrelated to mathematics and geometry in the Western tradition. Machaut only composed religious music.These groups were focused in expressing how one's childhood surroundings and circumstances created and regulated one's character.Almost every assisted chanson follows one of the three fixed forms: ballade, rondeau, or virelai.The songs are composed in a sophisticated style for a royal public with great creative aims and refined taste.The overarching theme was courtly love.The gradual transition to a chanson form in which four voices sing the same text, often in rhythmic copy but also in a harmonically (approaches indicate) style.

MORE ABOUT CHANSONCommon examples are Edith Piaf's La Vie En RoseHISTORYThe monophonic chanson, which dates back to the 12th century, rose to prominence with the trouvères of the 13th century and may still be found in the musician and poet Guillaume de Machaut's mid-14th-century lais (a verse-song form). Only the melodies remain. The monophonic chansons demonstrate the evolution of complicated musico-poetic forms derived from the songs of the trouvères' slightly older predecessors, the troubadours. These forms were later reduced to create the accompanying chanson's formes fixes ("fixed forms").
