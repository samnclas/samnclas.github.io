3. example of PARTITIVE PROPORTION and show a solution


A partitive proportion, or proportion by parts, describes a whole amount being divided or distributed into two or more unequal parts.

Example:
The ratio of boys to girls in a class is 3:5. If there are 48 students in the class, how many boys and girls are there?

Since we don't know the constant of proportionality, we let that as [tex]x[/tex], which means that
[tex]3x[/tex] is the number of boys
[tex]5x[/tex] is the number of girls

[tex]3x+5x=48[/tex]
[tex]8x=48[/tex]
[tex]x=6[/tex]

Therefore, there are [tex]3(6)=18[/tex] boys and there are [tex]5(6)=30[/tex] girls.

Notice that if we check the numbers back into the problem, [tex]18+30=48[/tex] students in the class.
