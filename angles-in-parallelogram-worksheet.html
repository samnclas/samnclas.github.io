25. Measure of one angle of a parallelogram is 2/3 of its measure of the adjacent angle. Measure of the smaller angle of the parallelogram is


Answer:

The smaller angle's measure is 72°.

Step-by-step explanation:

Property of the parallelogram with regards to its angles:

The adjacent or consecutive angles of a parallelogram are supplementary.

The sum of the measures of supplementary angles is 180°.

The adjacent/consecutive angles:

Bigger angle: x

Smaller angle: (2/3)x

Equation:

x + (2/3)x = 180°

Solve for the smaller angle, x:

(3)[x + (2/3)x = 180] (3)   ⇒ multiply each term by their LCD 3

3x + 2x = 540

5x/5 = 540/5

x = 108

Substitute 108 to x:

Bigger angle: x = 108°

Smaller angle: (2/3)(108) = 72°

The smaller angle's measure is 72°.

Check:

108° + 72° = 180°

180° = 180°  (True)


26. if two consecutive angles of a parallelogram are in the ratio 2:3, find the measure of each angle of the parallelogram.


Solution:

Let (x) be the common factor;

2:3 = 2x:3x
2x + 3x = 180
5x = 180
x = 36

Substitute;

2x = 2(36) = 72
3x = 3(36) = 108

Answer:

72 and 108.

29. The parallelogram a,b,c,d has three congruent angles. Calculate the measures of the angles of the parallelogram?​


Answer:

The measures of the angles of the parallelogram are 90 degrees.

Step-by-step explanation:

If three angles of the parallelogram are congruent (equal), then the fourth is opposite one of the 3, so it is also congruent

It means that the 4 angles are congruent.

The sum of the measures of the angles of a parallelogram is 360°, so the measure of each angle is: 360° : 4 = 90°

Angles of the Parallelogram

There are four interior angles inside a parallelogram. These angles have a sum of 360°. Based on the properties of the parallelogram, the opposite angles of the parallelogram are equal and congruent. Also, the consecutive angles of the parallelogram are supplementary. Supplementary angles are known as angles with a sum of 180°.
