9. Queenie borrowed a certain amount from a bank with interest at 6% annually. After 3 years, she paid back P25,400 to the bank for principal plus interest. How much was borrowed?​


Answer:

We can start by using the formula for simple interest:

Simple Interest = Principal x Rate x Time

where Principal is the amount borrowed, Rate is the interest rate as a decimal, and Time is the length of time in years.

Let x be the amount borrowed. Then, the simple interest after 3 years is:

Simple Interest = x × 0.06 × 3 = 0.18x

The total amount paid back after 3 years is the sum of the principal and the simple interest:

Total Amount = Principal + Simple Interest = x + 0.18x = 1.18x

We know that Queenie paid back P25,400, so we can set up an equation:

1.18x = 25400

Solving for x, we get:

x = 25400 ÷ 1.18 ≈ 21,525.42

Therefore, Queenie borrowed approximately P21,525.42 from the bank.
