21. persuasive essay convincing people to stop biases and prejudices​


It is no secret that biases and prejudices exist in many societies, and this can lead to hatred, violence and ultimately injustice. Despite sympathy and agreement by most people that these issues must be stopped, they remain prevalent, often due to ignorance and misunderstandings. In order to eradicate bias and prejudice, we must understand why it exists in the first place, and ways we can go about convincing people to stop holding these views.

At its core, bias and prejudice is born out of the feeling of being ‘threatened’. When people encounter a new culture, religion or skin colour, they can often become fearful and unsure of their own place in the world. This then encourages people to make baseless assumptions and judgements, which can lead to hatred and discrimination. It is therefore important that we work to reduce these feelings of fear by addressing the issues that cause them.

The best way to do this is through education. By providing people with information about different cultures, we can help to broaden the minds of those around us and encourage them to embrace diversity. This can be done through educational institutions, where awareness can be spread about the importance of tolerance, as well as cultural events and activities which bring people together. Additionally, encouraging people to engage in open discussions about bias and prejudice allows for an environment of understanding to form.

In addition to education, creating a positive environment of acceptance is another key factor in convincing people to stop biases and prejudices. We must show people that by embracing diversity, we stand to gain much more than simply understanding different cultures. This can be done by creating supportive communities and networks that embrace people of all backgrounds, as well as actively challenging biased attitudes when we see them.

Ultimately, in order to eliminate bias and prejudice, it is important that we work together to create an environment where all people are valued and respected. By addressing the root cause of the issue, spreading awareness and creating a positive social atmosphere, we can make progress toward eradicating these views and making our societies fairer and more equal.

29. persuasive essay convincing ​


Answer:

Writing a persuasive essay is like being a lawyer arguing a case before a jury. The writer takes a stand on an issue—either “for” or “against”—and builds the strongest possible argument to win over the reader.

In a persuasive essay, it’s the writer’s job to convince the reader to accept a particular point of view or take a specific action. Persuasive essays require good research, awareness of the reader’s biases, and a solid understanding of both sides of the issue. A good persuasive essay demonstrates not only why the writer’s opinion is correct, but also why the opposing view is incorrect.

Persuasive writing is a fixture of modern life—found in advertising, newspaper editorials, blogs, and political speeches. Often persuasive writing assignments and test prompts concern contemporary issues, for example: “The school board is debating on whether or not to ban cell phone use in school. Write an essay convincing the board to adopt your position.” As shown in this persuasive writing prompt, the main purpose is not to inform, but to “persuade” or “convince” an audience (the school board) to think or act a certain way.
