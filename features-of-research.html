9. Differentiate the language of research with the language features of argumentative essays. ​


Answer:

Informative Essays

Informative essays are a type of writing that explains a certain issue or a topic. An informative writer

should always maintain an objective, neutral tone throughout the essay, or they shouldn’t try to argue

one side over another. The author of an informative essay is kind of like a reporter at a newspaper: only

there to state the facts, not to offer any sort of opinion. In this type of an essay, the writer should refrain

from pointing out his personal ideas and beliefs. For SAGE testing, an informative essay is three

paragraphs long: one introduction paragraph containing the central idea of the paper, one body

paragraph that contains enough facts and information to cover the topic, and a conclusion that briefly

summarizes all the points explained in the body. Real writers use as many body paragraphs as they need

to explain the topic they’re writing about, but for the purposes of SAGE testing, stick to just one body  paragraph

15. what are the features of marketing research​


Answer:

Systematic approach: Marketing research follows a systematic approach, starting with the formulation of research objectives, followed by data collection, analysis, and interpretation.

Objective-oriented: Marketing research is conducted with specific objectives in mind. These objectives may include understanding consumer behavior, evaluating the effectiveness of marketing campaigns, or identifying opportunities in the market.

Data-driven: Marketing research is based on data collected from various sources such as surveys, interviews, focus groups, and secondary research. The data is then analyzed to draw conclusions and make recommendations.

Analytical: Marketing research involves the use of statistical and analytical tools to interpret the data collected. This helps to identify patterns, trends, and insights that can be used to inform marketing strategies.

Action-oriented: Marketing research is designed to provide actionable insights that can be used to make informed business decisions. The research findings should be relevant, timely, and useful for the decision-making process.

Continuous process: Marketing research is an ongoing process, as market conditions, consumer preferences, and competition are constantly evolving. It is important for companies to regularly conduct research to stay up-to-date and adapt to changes in the market.

The LRT (Light Rail Transit) and MRT (Metro Rail Transit) are two rapid transit systems in the National Capital Region (NCR) of the Philippines. While both are similar in terms of function, there are several differences between them. Here are some of the features of the LRT and MRT:

Features of LRT:

- The LRT has two lines, LRT-1 and LRT-2, with a combined length of around 33 kilometers and 31 stations.

- The LRT-1 line runs from Baclaran to Roosevelt, while the LRT-2 line runs from Recto to Santolan.

- The trains used in the LRT system are smaller and lighter than those used in the MRT system, with a capacity of around 1,000 passengers per train.

- The LRT system operates from 5:00 AM to 10:00 PM on weekdays and 5:00 AM to 9:30 PM on weekends and holidays.

- The fare for the LRT depends on the distance traveled, with a minimum fare of Php 13.

Features of MRT:

- The MRT has a single line with a length of around 16.9 kilometers and 13 stations.

- The MRT line runs from North Avenue in Quezon City to Taft Avenue in Pasay City.

- The trains used in the MRT system are larger and heavier than those used in the LRT system, with a capacity of around 1,800 passengers per train.

- The MRT system operates from 4:30 AM to 10:30 PM on weekdays and 5:30 AM to 9:30 PM on weekends and holidays.

- The fare for the MRT also depends on the distance traveled, with a minimum fare of Php 13.

In terms of differences, the MRT system is generally considered to be faster and more efficient than the LRT system. This is partly due to the larger and more powerful trains used in the MRT system, as well as the fact that the MRT line has fewer stations than the LRT lines. However, the MRT system is also more prone to breakdowns and service interruptions due to its age and the high number of passengers it serves.

Compared to the Shinkansen or bullet train in Japan, the LRT and MRT systems in the Philippines are relatively slower and less advanced. The Shinkansen has a top speed of around 320 kilometers per hour, while the LRT and MRT systems in the Philippines have a top speed of around 60 kilometers per hour. Additionally, the Shinkansen has a much lower rate of breakdowns and service interruptions, with trains running on time almost 100% of the time. The Shinkansen is also more expensive than the LRT and MRT systems, with ticket prices varying depending on the distance traveled, but generally ranging from around ¥10,000 to ¥20,000 (Php 4,800 to Php 9,600).
