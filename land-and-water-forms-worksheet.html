What is a Shadow?

A shadow is a dark area where light from a light source is blocked by an opaque object. It occupies all of the three-dimensional volume behind an object with light in front of it. The cross section of a shadow is a two-dimensional silhouette, or a reverse projection of the object blocking the light.

The rain bands, eye, and eyewall make up a tropical cyclone's core components. In the northern hemisphere (and clockwise in the southern hemisphere), air spirals inward toward the center and then out the top in the opposite direction.

Tropical Cyclones have:

It's Eye

The hurricane's center The calmest region of a storm and it typically forms when maximum sustained wind speeds exceed 74 mph.

Its Eyewall

The eyewall is where the powerful wind can approach the closest. Strong winds and heavy rain are typically produced by the ring of tall thunderstorms that make up the eyewall.

Rain bands

Clouds in curved bands and thunderstorms that spiral out from the eye wall are seen. These bands have the power to generate tornadoes, as well as strong gusts of wind and rain.
