15. Discuss the types of questions asked in the survey questionnaire.


Survey questionnaires can be used to collect data on a variety of topics, such as attitudes, opinions, behaviors, and demographics. The types of questions asked in a survey questionnaire can be broadly classified into the following categories:

Open-ended questions: These questions allow respondents to provide free-form answers. Open-ended questions are useful when the researcher wants to explore a topic in-depth and gather rich qualitative data. However, they can be time-consuming to analyze.

Closed-ended questions: These questions provide a set of response options for the respondent to choose from. Closed-ended questions are useful for collecting quantitative data that can be easily analyzed. Common types of closed-ended questions include multiple choice, rating scales, and ranking questions.

Likert scale questions: These are a type of closed-ended question that asks respondents to rate their agreement or disagreement with a statement on a scale, typically ranging from "strongly agree" to "strongly disagree". Likert scales are commonly used to measure attitudes and opinions.

Demographic questions: These questions gather information about the respondent's background, such as age, gender, income, and education level. Demographic questions are important for segmenting the data and understanding how different groups of respondents answered the survey questions.

Matrix questions: These questions ask respondents to rate multiple items on the same scale. Matrix questions are useful for collecting data on related topics, such as customer satisfaction with different aspects of a product or service.

Dichotomous questions: These are closed-ended questions that have only two response options, such as yes/no questions. Dichotomous questions are useful for collecting binary data.

Semantic differential questions: These are a type of rating scale question that asks respondents to rate a concept or object on a set of bipolar adjectives, such as good-bad, fast-slow, and expensive-cheap. Semantic differential questions are useful for measuring attitudes and perceptions.

When designing a survey questionnaire, it is important to choose the appropriate types of questions based on the research objectives and the target audience.

Do you just create a product and send it out? Or, do you take the trouble to analyze who your customers are, what they need, and how to best meet their needs?
