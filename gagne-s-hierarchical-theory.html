3. Creat an essay about hierarchical theory?


Answer:

Hierarchical theory is a widely accepted psychological theory that suggests that human needs are arranged in a hierarchical order, with basic needs at the bottom and higher needs at the top. The theory was developed by Abraham Maslow in the mid-twentieth century, and it remains a cornerstone of psychology and human motivation theory.

According to the theory, human needs can be categorized into five basic levels, arranged in a pyramid-like structure. The first and most basic level is physiological needs, such as food, water, and shelter. These needs are the most fundamental and essential for human survival. Once these needs are met, individuals can move on to the next level, which is safety needs, such as protection from harm and security.

The third level is social needs, such as the need for love, belongingness, and social interaction. After these needs are satisfied, individuals can move on to the fourth level, which is esteem needs, such as the need for self-esteem and the recognition and respect of others. Finally, the highest level of needs is self-actualization, which refers to the need for personal growth, self-fulfillment, and the realization of one's full potential.

Maslow's hierarchy of needs has been widely adopted by psychologists and other social scientists to understand human motivation, behavior, and personality. The theory has important implications for individuals, organizations, and society as a whole. For individuals, understanding their needs can help them identify what motivates them and what they need to be happy and fulfilled in life. For organizations, the theory can help managers understand what motivates employees and how to create a work environment that fosters employee satisfaction and productivity.

However, the hierarchical theory has also been criticized for being too simplistic and for not accounting for cultural and individual differences in needs and values. Some psychologists argue that there is no clear hierarchy of needs and that different needs may be more or less important depending on the individual's personality and cultural background. Additionally, some critics argue that the theory places too much emphasis on individual needs and does not account for the importance of collective needs and social justice.

Despite these criticisms, Maslow's hierarchy of needs remains a fundamental theory in psychology and has inspired numerous studies and applications. The theory provides a framework for understanding human behavior and motivation and can help individuals and organizations develop strategies to promote personal and professional growth and fulfillment.

The hierarchical theory is a concept that has been widely studied in various fields, including psychology, sociology, and management. It proposes that individuals or groups are arranged in a structured order, where each level is subordinate to the one above it. This structure is often depicted as a pyramid, with the highest level representing those with the most power and authority.

One of the earliest proponents of hierarchical theory was the philosopher Aristotle, who believed that humans naturally organize themselves into hierarchies based on their abilities and virtues. Later, sociologist Max Weber further developed the concept, arguing that hierarchies are necessary for the efficient functioning of complex organizations.

In terms of management, a hierarchical theory has been used to design organizational structures that aim to optimize productivity and efficiency. This is typically achieved by dividing tasks and responsibilities into distinct levels, with each level having its own set of duties and reporting lines. It is believed that this approach helps to clarify roles and responsibilities while also promoting accountability.

However, the hierarchical theory has also been criticized for its potential to create rigid and inflexible structures. Critics argue that this can stifle creativity and innovation, as well as limit opportunities for upward mobility. Additionally, hierarchical structures can often lead to power imbalances, with those at the top having more control and influence than those lower down the pyramid.

Despite these criticisms, the hierarchical theory remains an important concept in many fields. By understanding how individuals and groups are organized, we can better design systems and structures that promote efficiency, productivity, and fairness.

7. gagne's condition of learning in cognitive strategies​


Answer:

Cognitive Strategies

Gagné defines cognitive strategies as enabling learners to “exercise some degree of control over the processes involved in attending, perceiving, encoding, remembering, and thinking” (Gagné, 1984). In other words, the student employs personal ways to learn, think, guide, and act.  Instructional design which promotes internal and external learning activities is the core of Gagné’s cognitive perspective (Richey, 2000).

Cognitive Strategy is an internal process by which the learner controls his/her own ways of thinking and learning. Example: Engaging in self-testing to decide how much study is needed; knowing what sorts of questions to ask to best define a domain of knowledge; ability to form a mental model of the problem.

What is a concept map?

A concept map is a diagram that shows the relationships between different ideas. This helps you understand how they’re connected.

Every concept map — whether it’s simple or complex — is made up of two key elements:

CONCEPTS: These are typically represented by circles, ovals, or boxes and are called “nodes.”

RELATIONSHIPS: These are represented by arrows that connect the concepts, and the arrows often include a connecting word or verb (but they don’t have to). These arrows are called “cross-links.”
