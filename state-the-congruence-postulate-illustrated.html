4. Illustrate the three triangle congruence postulates?​


Here are the three triangle congruence postulates:

1.) Side-Side-Side (SSS) Postulate: If the three sides of one triangle are congruent to the three sides of another triangle, then the two triangles are congruent.

Triangle ABC and triangle XYZ are congruent because AB = XY, BC = YZ, and AC = XZ.

2.) Side-Angle-Side (SAS) Postulate: If two sides and the included angle of one triangle are congruent to two sides and the included angle of another triangle, then the two triangles are congruent.

Triangle ABC and triangle XYZ are congruent because AB = XY, BC = YZ, and the included angle between AB and BC is congruent to the included angle between XY and YZ.

3.) Angle-Side-Angle (ASA) Postulate: If two angles and the included side of one triangle are congruent to two angles and the included side of another triangle, then the two triangles are congruent.

Triangle ABC and triangle XYZ are congruent because the included angle between sides AB and AC is congruent to the included angle between sides XY and XZ, angle B is congruent to angle Y, and angle C is congruent to angle Z.

Note: The congruent sides and angles are marked with the same number of arcs or lines.

20. Illustrate the three triangle congruence postulates​


Answer:

If the hypotenuse and one of the legs (sides) of a right triangle are congruent to hypotenuse and corresponding leg of the other right triangle, the two triangles are said to be congruent. If all three sides of a triangle are congruent to corresponding three sides of other triangle then the two triangles are congruent.

Answer:

Yes congruent when the criterias are met. This criterias are as follows.

(1) SAS congruence criteria:

In the acronym SAS, the two S's stands for sides whereas the A stands for angle of the triangle. Thus, if two corresponding sides and angle of two or more triangles are equal, the triangles are congruent by SAS (side-angle-side) criteria.

Please note that if BC = PQ or any other non-corresponding sides in a triangle will not be congruent under SAS congruence criteria.

(2) SSS congruence criteria:

In this congruence criteria, if all the corresponding sides of a triangle are equal to each other, then they are congruent triangles.

For example in the above picture, AB = PQ, BC = QR, AC = PR, therefore we can say that they are congruent under side-side-side or SSS criteria. However same in case 1, if they are non-corresponding sidea then the trianglea will not be congruent.

(3) ASA congruence criteria:

If two corresponding angles and one corresponding sides of a triangle are equal, then they are congruent under ASA congruence criteria. Same in cases (1) and (2), if they are not corresponding they will not be congruent. This is true for all other cases. An example is given below.

(4) AAS congruence criteria:

It may look same like ASA, however the main difference is as follows:

● ASA means that two corresponding angles of a triangle are equal and the side included in them is equal to the other triangle.

● In AAS, there is no such rule. Any random side and angle if are congruent, then the triangles will also be congruent to each other.

(5) RHS congruence criteria:

It is true for only right triangles. It stands for right angle-hypotenuse-side congruence criteria.

It means if the corresponding hypotenuse and one side of two or more triangles are equal, and they are both right angled triangle, then they are congruent to each other. Hypotenuse is the longest side of any right angled triangle. For example, the above picture the shown two triangles are congruent to each other.
