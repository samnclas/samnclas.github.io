5. 8. Which of the following volcanoes is inactive or dormant?a. Mt. ApoC. Mt. Urotb. Mt. IrayaD. Mt. Hibok-hibok​


Inactive or Dormant Volcanoes

Out of all the volcanoes shown on the choices, the only inactive volcano is Mount Urot. Mount Apo is classified as a potentially active volcano, while Mounts Iraya and Hibok-Hibok are classified as active volcanoes. Therefore, the correct answer is letter C.

Why Mount Urot is considered as an inactive volcano?

Mount Urot is located in the province of Sulu, and scientists considered it as an inactive volcano because the volcanic belt in the province is known for monogenetic cone eruptions – an eruption that only happens once and will never happen again.

What is an active volcano?

We classify a volcano as an active volcano if it erupted in the last 600 years, as proven by historical records. In the absence of historical records, geological deposits yielding ages within the Holocene epoch can also be considered.

What is an inactive volcano?

An inactive volcano is a volcano that last erupted hundreds of thousands and even millions of years ago. These volcanoes no longer have the chance to erupt again as their magma chambers already cooled down.

What are dormant volcanoes?

Dormant volcanoes are volcanoes that have not erupted in the last hundreds of years. However, volcanologists today no longer use this term. They prefer using the terms active, potentially active, and inactive.

Explanation:

In the Philippines, PHIVOLCS classify volcanoes into three categories – active, inactive, and potentially active. An active volcano should have a historical eruption with accompanying records or witnesses. Alternatively, the geologic deposits should be dated at least 10,000 years old or younger, well within the bounds of the Holocene epoch. Potentially active volcanoes, on the other hand, do not have any historical eruptions or any geologic deposits that is Holocene in age, but solfataric activity is present in the volcano. Inactive volcanoes lack any historical eruptions or geological deposits that are Holocene in age, and they also lack any solfataric activity. Also, deep gullies due to severe erosion are present along its flanks.
