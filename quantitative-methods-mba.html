3. 1.name and briefly define the quantitative methods of control.2. what are non-quantitive methods of control?​


The quantitative methods of control:

1. Correlation

2. Descriptive

3. Comparative Causal

4. Comparative

Non-quantitative methods of control:

1. Phenomenological Research

2. Grounded Theory

3. Ethnography

4. Case Study

5. Narrative Research

EXPLANATION:

The quantitative methods of control:

1. Correlation

The correlational method is one of the various quantitative research methods used in evaluation. Mainly to detect the extent to which variations in a factor are related to variations in one or more other factors based on the correlation coefficient.

2. Descriptive

The descriptive method is a type of quantitative research method with a problem formulation that integrates research to explore or photograph social situations that will be thoroughly, broadly, and deeply examined. Various types of quantitative research methods such as descriptive aim to systematically describe facts or characteristics of certain populations or certain fields factually and accurately.

3. Comparative Causal

The comparative causal research method is one of the various quantitative research methods. The popular name for these types of quantitative research methods is ex-post facto. The comparative causal method is used in evaluation to determine possible cause-and-effect relationships.

4. Comparative

Various types of quantitative research methods such as comparative ones function to compare two or more treatments of a variable, or several variables at once. The purpose of various quantitative research methods such as comparative is to see the differences of two or more situations, events, activities, or programs.

5. Experiment

The experimental research method is one of the various quantitative research methods. This quantitative research was conducted to test whether or not the experimental variables were effective. Experimental research is usually more widely used in the exact field. There are two types of experimental research, pseudo and real.

6. Survey

The survey method is used in evaluation to make systematic, factual and accurate recording of facts and characteristics of a particular population or area. Various quantitative research methods such as surveys are used to obtain or collect information about large populations.

7. Inferential

Inferential is a type of quantitative research method that analyzes the relationship between variables by testing hypotheses. Thus, the research conclusions go far beyond just providing quantitative data. In inferential research, it can discuss the magnitude of the chance of error in drawing conclusions.

Non-quantitative methods of control:

1. Phenomenological Research

In this quantitative research, researchers collect data through participant observation.

2. Grounded Theory

In this qualitative research, the researcher draws generalizations on what is observed or analyzed inductively.

3. Ethnography

Ethnographic qualitative research is research on group culture through interviews and observations.

4. Case Study

In this qualitative research, the researcher explores an event, program, process, or activity.

5. Narrative Research

In this qualitative research, the researcher conducts a study to obtain historical data which is then compiled into a chronological narrative report.
