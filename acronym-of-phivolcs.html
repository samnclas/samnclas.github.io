1. What is the acronym for PHIVOLCS?​


Acronym Meaning: PHIVOLCS

The acronym PHIVOLCS stands for Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology. It is the leading geological agency in the Philippines, and they are mandated to study volcanoes, earthquakes, and other related phenomena.

History of PHIVOLCS

The history of the institute can be traced back in 1952, one year after the disastrous eruption of Mount Hibok-Hibok in Camiguin. Due to the lack of government agency that can monitor the destructive potential of Philippine Volcanoes, the Philippine government decided to pass R.A. 766, which created the Commission on Volcanology (COMVOL), the predecessor to the institute. In 1982, a reorganization of government agencies began, and COMVOL was renamed to PHIVOLC. The transfer of seismology from PAGASA to PHIVOLC in 1984 gave way to the creation of the present institute we know today – PHIVOLCS.

Where can you find the office of PHIVOLCS?

The office of PHIVOLCS is currently located along C. P. Garcia Avenue, inside the University of the Philippines Diliman Campus in Quezon City. Dr. Renato U. Solidum, Jr. serves as the officer-in-charge.

27. true or false the phivolcs uses the phivolcs earthquake intensity scale to describe the intensity of earthquakes in the philippines​


Answer:

PHIVOLCS Earthquake Intensity Scale

The answer is true. PHIVOLCS uses their own PHIVOLCS Earthquake Intensity Scale to describe the intensity of earthquakes happening in the Philippines. This scale is different from the Mercalli Intensity Scale, because the maximum number of intensity is only X or 10. The Mercalli Intensity Scale can reach up to intensity XII or 12.

Explanation:

Many people get confused when they hear the terms intensity and magnitude. However, we can easily differentiate these two. Intensity refers to the amount of damage that a locality received after an earthquake. In short, it measures the earthquake’s impact to humans. Magnitude, on the other hand, tells us about the energy released by the earthquake.
