18. The following transactions would change the accounting equation, EXCEPT one.Which is the exception?a. Purchase of land on creditb. Investment of additional capitalc. Sale of goods for cashd. Deposit of cash in bank


Subject Economics

Answer:

The correct answer among the given listed choices is letter d. Transactions choices from a, b and c change the accounting equation except deposit of cash in bank.

ACCOUNTING TRANSACTIONS

From the above transactions, let's provide the accounting entry of each transactions to further understand.

a. Purchase of land on credit

This transaction will have an effect on accounting equation by increasing the asset account and increasing the payable account.

Accounting Entry

Debit    Land

Credit   Accounts Payable/ or any payable account appropriate

b. Investment of additional capital

This transaction will have an effect on accounting equation by increasing the asset account and increasing the equity account.

Accounting Entry

Debit    Asset or any investment appropriate account

Credit   Owners' Capital

c. Sale of goods for cash

Referring from the entry, this transaction has an accounting effect.

Accounting Entry

Debit    Cash

Debit    Cost of Good Sold

Credit   Merchandise Inventory

Credit   Sales

d. Deposit of cash in bank

Observing the entry, cash on hand recorded is only reversed to become a cash in bank since it was deposited. There is a reversing entry  to transfer the recorded cash on hand to cash on hand. Therefore, there is no change in the accounting equation. There is only a change in the account title.

Accounting Entry

Debit    Cash in Bank

Credit   Cash on Hand
