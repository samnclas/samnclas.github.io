25. what is the purpose of peer pressure in concept paper ​

Explanation:

Peer pressure is the influence that people of the same social group can have on one another. It can be a powerful force in shaping behavior, attitudes, and values. The purpose of peer pressure can vary depending on the context and the specific situation.

In some cases, peer pressure can be positive and help individuals to make good decisions or to try new things that they might not have otherwise considered. For example, peer pressure might encourage someone to study harder or to get involved in a new activity.

On the other hand, peer pressure can also be negative and lead people to make choices that are harmful or risky. For example, peer pressure might lead someone to engage in risky behaviors like drug use or reckless driving.

In a concept paper, the purpose of discussing peer pressure might be to explore how it can influence people's behavior and decision-making, or to consider the ways in which peer pressure can be managed or mitigated.
