7. Write a paragraph of four to six sentences explaining how geography influences regional cuisine. Think about the products grown in the region and what countries or bodies of water these regions border. Mention at least two regions discussed in this lesson. Please help its for 20pts!!


Answer:

Geography plays a significant role in shaping regional cuisine as it determines the types of products grown, availability of resources, and influences trade and cultural exchange. Regions that border a particular country or a body of water often incorporate ingredients from their neighbors' cuisine. For example, in Southeast Asia, rice is a staple food, while spices, coconut, and seafood are popular ingredients due to the region's tropical climate and proximity to the ocean. In contrast, in Central Europe, meat and dairy products are prominent due to the abundant farmland and cooler climate. In the Caribbean, regional cuisine features a fusion of African, European, and indigenous influences, with staples such as plantains, cassava, and seafood. These are just a few examples of how geography and climate shape regional cuisine.

24. ano ang regional geography? at ano ang kahalagahan nito? (tagalog)​


Answer:

Ang regional geography ay isang sangay ng heograpiya na tumutukoy sa pag-aaral ng mga rehiyon sa mundo, kabilang ang kanilang mga pisikal na katangian, ekonomiya, kultura, at mga tao.

Ang kahalagahan ng regional geography ay ang pagbibigay ng pangkalahatang pag-unawa sa mga rehiyonal na konteksto at ang mga sangkap na bumubuo sa mga ito. Ito ay nagbibigay ng mga impormasyon at kaalaman tungkol sa mga pagbabago sa rehiyon, mga krisis, mga oportunidad at mga hamon na kinakaharap ng mga tao sa iba't ibang lugar. Sa gayon, ang regional geography ay makatutulong sa pagbuo ng mga desisyon at polisiya na nakabatay sa katotohanan at pangangailangan ng mga tao sa bawat rehiyon.

Geography is a major component in regional cuisine. For example, in the Mediterranean region, the countries in the area border the Mediterranean Sea, making seafood dishes a staple component of many dishes. The hot, dry climate of the Mediterranean region also allows for a wide variety of produce to be grown, making vegetables, fruits, and grains such as olives, oranges, and wheat a part of the staples of Mediterranean cuisine. Similarly, the Latin American region is influenced by the cuisines of the many nations that make up the area, as well as its proximity to the Pacific and Atlantic Oceans.
