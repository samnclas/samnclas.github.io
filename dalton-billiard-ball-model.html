8. compare dalton's model with bohr's model of the atom​


Answer:

Explanation:

Dalton's model explained that atoms were tiny invisibly, indestructible particles; And that each had a certain mass, size, and chemical behavior that determined by what kind of element they were. He called his model the " Billiard Ball model" because he thought that atoms looked like billiard balls from pool. John Dalton theorized that matter was made up of many tiny particles called atoms that had no parts. And he thought that atoms looked like Billiard Balls.

Niels Bohr changed the atomic theory by creating the Bohr model of the atom.  He improved upon previous models such as the cubic model, the plum-pudding model, the Saturnian model, and the Rutherford model. His model represents the atom as a small, positively charged nucleus surrounded by electrons that travel in circular orbits around the nucleus which is akin to the solar system.

20. Click the prediction tab and check the first model for the atom, the Billiard Ball. Who suggested this model and what were his conclusions about the structure of an atom?


The first model for the atom, the Billiard Ball was suggested by John

Dalton, and his conclusions about the structure of an atom is that the atom is the smallest unit making up an element that cannot be subdivided.

Further explanation

The atomic theory was first put forward by Greek scientists who stated that every substance has the smallest particles, so that the term atom appears which means it cannot be divided.

From this John Dalton put forward the hypothesis that the atom is a solid sphere like a very small shot put ball or a bowling ball based on Dalton's hobby in bowling

From the experiments conducted, Dalton gave several conclusions about the atom

1. The elements are composed of atoms which cannot be subdivided 2. Atoms that make up the same elements have the same properties, mass, and size 3. Compounds are composed of two or more atoms in a fixed ratio 4. In chemical reactions, atoms after and before a reaction cannot be destroyed, only separation and reassembly occurrences
