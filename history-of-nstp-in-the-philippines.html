1. what is the history of nstp​


The National Service Training Program (NSTP), is a civic education and defense preparedness program students instituted by the Government of the Philippines on 5 January 2000 by virtue of Republic Act 9163, otherwise known as the "National Service Training Program (NSTP) Act of 2001."

2. what is the history of NSTP?​


HISTORY

The National Service Training Program (NSTP) was implemented by the University of the Philippines (UP) Diliman in accordance with the Republic Act (RA) 9163, an Act Establishing The National Service Training Program (NSTP) for Tertiary Level Students, enabling the government to create a program that will encourage the youth to actively participate in civic welfare and literacy initiatives as well as defend the country in times of war. It was, however, only in 2011 that UP Diliman paid attention to unifying the different NSTP initiatives of the various colleges and units, after the creation of the Office of the Director of NSTP (system level) and the appointment of an NSTP Coordinator for UP Diliman.

The NSTP Diliman was officially established by virtue of a decision made by the Board of Regents during its 1288th BOR Meeting on June 20, 2013. Its functions are to administer, manage, and coordinate all NSTP offerings and activities in UP Diliman in accordance with RA 9163 of 2001 and its duly approved implementing rules and regulations.

In 2016, following a reorganization of the Office of the Vice Chancellor for Academic Affairs, the NSTP Diliman was created to be headed by a director and a deputy director for National Service Reserve Corps (NSRC).

NSTP PROGRAM IN THE PHILIPPINES

1. The Civic Welfare Training Service course, mandated by Republic Act No. 9163, also known as the National Service Training Act of 2001, seeks to improve students' civic consciousness "through fostering the principles of service and patriotism" while undertaking Civic Welfare Training Service (CWTS). NSTP1 presents themes in large campus sessions that capitalize on students' excitement and idealism for nation-building, leadership, and civic action. It prepares students for genuine community service in NSTP 2 by combining active reflection in a creative dynamic learning environment.

2. The goal of this program is to acknowledge the importance of youth in nation-building, to raise awareness among young, and to nurture their physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-being. It will instill patriotism and nationalism in the youth and encourage their participation in public and civic issues. In order to achieve these aims, the nation's most important resource, the youth, must be inspired, trained, organized, and mobilized in military training, literacy, civic welfare, and/or comparable activities to the nation's service. The primary role of the government, as stated in RA 9163, is to serve and protect its inhabitants. In turn, it must be the responsibility of all citizens to protect the security of the state, and the government may ask each citizen to provide personal, military, or civil service in order to meet this obligation.

11. why the NSTP - MAPUA office created the 3 component of NSTP​


Answer:

The Republic Act No. 9163, which is the act that establishes the National Service Training Program for tertiary level students, stipulated that the program will have three components tertiary level students can sign up for with the purpose of enhancing civic consciousness and defense preparedness in the youth by developing the ethics of service and patriotism. These programs are the following:

Reserve-Officers’ Training Corps or ROTC which is a program institutionalized under Sections 38 and 39 of Republic Act No. 7077 intended to provide military training to tertiary level students in order to motivate, train, organize and mobilize them as part of national defense preparednessLiteracy Training Service or LTS which is a program developed to train students to become teachers of reading, writing, and number skills to school children, out of school youth, and other segments of society who are in need of their serviceCivic Welfare Training Service or CWTS refers to programs or activities contributory to the general welfare and the enhancement of life for the members of the community or the betterment of its facilities, especially those devoted to improving health, education, environment, entrepreneurship, safety, recreation and morals of the Filipino citizens.

Mapua, just like other universities, is following the rules and regulations set by the Republic Act No. 9163.

HISTORY OF NSTP

The Years of Spain During the time that the Philippines was a Spanish colony, the University of Sto. Tomas had a training program that was like what we now call ROTC. Modern ROTC Begins Most modern writers, on the other hand, think that real ROTC in this country didn't start until 1912 when military training began at the University of the Philippines.

ROTC in World War II: According to the records, ROTC graduates were called to the colors in 1942 and showed their worth on the battlefield during the occupation. Executive Order No. 59, which was about training after the war, said that all colleges and universities had to offer the ROTC course.

POST MARCOS ERA

ROTC is being replaced by the CMT Years or Citizen Military Training.

The Return of ROTC, or the AFP Reservist Act, is Republic Act 7077.

PRESENT DAY2001's ROTC Crisis ROTC is in danger of dying out because of student protests.Must-Not-Any-More ROTC was no longer required in the Philippines, and the National Service Reserve Corps was set up instead (RA 9163).

Significance of the NSTP

Being a student, you are expected to experience, be trained, and feel what is the real world. The theoretical world of going to school should be verified at least as early time as possible. This is what the community immersion had aimed for in the first place. The student would have inspiration from their small experience and observation. By doing so, we will let them feel the responsibility for being a future adult citizen. Maybe one of them will be inspired to do something not just for their own success but for the benefit of the nation.

NSTP stands for National Service Training Program. Some of the programs it offers are ROTC, CWTS, and LTS. Its goal is to promote and integrate values education, transformational leadership, the spirit of patriotism and nationalism, and sustainable social mobilization for youth development, community building, and national security.

The National Service Training Program (NSTP) is a program that helps young people become more civically aware and better prepared for defense by teaching them the values of service and patriotism while they train in one of the three (3) parts of the program. These different parts are all made to make it easier for young people to get involved in helping the community.

Under Republic Act (RA) No. 9163, all college students must take part in the National Service Training Program (NSTP). The law gives young people a chance to directly help build the country. Under the program, Lasallian students in their first year can take either the Reserve Officers' Training Corps (ROTC) or the Civic Welfare Service Training Service (CWTS).
