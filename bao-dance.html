1. What is the literal translation of the term "Bao"? Why Do you think is the dance called "Bao dance"?​


Answer:

What is Bao Dance?

One of the traditional dances in the Philippines is Bao Dance. This is also known as Magbabao where "bao" means a coconut shell.

This dance is called as that because dancers use coconut shells as props. Coconut shells are cut in halves and they are secured onto the dancers' hands, knees, elbows and back. It is performed by hitting one coconut shell with the others. Dancers sometimes hit the ones on the hands, sometimes the ones on the body, and sometimes the shells worn by another performer, all in time to a fast drumbeat.

Explanation:

How did it originate?

It is danced to mimic the early battle against Christians and Muslim Moros during the time of Spanish colonization.

How it is performed?

This is performed by all-male dancers who wear blue pants to represent the Christians and red pant for the Muslim Moros. All dancers have coconut shells mounted on their body.

This dance form is also known as "Maglalatik" dance as the prize for winning the war was latik. Latik is the residue obtained after boiling the coconut milk.

15. What is the difference between the Bao dance and the Maglalatik?​


The Maglalatik (also known as Manlalatik or Magbabao) is a folk dance from the Philippines. Coconut shell halves are secured onto the dancers' hands and on vests upon which are hung four or six more coconut shell halves. The dancers perform the dance by hitting one coconut shell with the other; sometimes the ones on the hands, the ones on the body, or the shells worn by another performer, all in time to a fast drumbeat.

The Maglalatik (also known as Manlalatik or Magbabao) is a folk dance from the Philippines. Coconut shell halves are secured onto the dancers' hands and on vests upon which are hung four or six more coconut shell halves. The dancers perform the dance by hitting one coconut shell with the other; sometimes the ones on the hands, the ones on the body, or the shells worn by another performer, all in time to a fast drumbeat.The dance is intended to impress the viewer with the great skill of the dancer. In some "Filipino Martial Arts" (FMA) circles, it is noted that the Maglalatik "consists of a trapping and boxing method hidden in a dance."
