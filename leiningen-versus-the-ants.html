6. differentiate magma versus lava versus volcanic ash ​


Answer:

Magma, lava, and volcanic ash are all related to volcanic activity, but they have distinct differences. Here's how they differ:

Magma - Magma is a molten rock that is located beneath the Earth's surface. It is formed by the melting of rock due to high temperature and pressure, and it can contain gases, minerals, and other materials. Magma can rise towards the surface and can cause volcanic eruptions when it reaches the Earth's surface.

Lava - Lava is molten rock that has erupted onto the Earth's surface. It can flow or explode out of a volcano during an eruption, and it can form a variety of landforms such as volcanoes, lava domes, and lava fields. Lava is typically more viscous and slower moving than magma due to the cooling and crystallization that occurs as it moves towards the surface.

Volcanic Ash - Volcanic ash is a mixture of small, solid particles and gases that are ejected into the atmosphere during a volcanic eruption. It is formed by the explosive fragmentation of lava and rock, and it can travel long distances from the eruption site. Volcanic ash can cause a range of hazards, including respiratory problems, damage to crops, and disruption to transportation and communication systems.

In summary, magma is molten rock beneath the Earth's surface, lava is molten rock that has erupted onto the Earth's surface, and volcanic ash is a mixture of solid particles and gases that are ejected into the atmosphere during a volcanic eruption.
