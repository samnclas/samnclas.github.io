14. If 4.27 grams of sucrose, C12H22O11, are dissolved in 15.2 grams of water, what will be the boiling point of the resulting solution? (Kb for water = 0.512 oC/m)a.99.626 oCb.101.42 oCc.100.73 oCd.100.42 oCe.101.64 oC​


Given:

mass of solute = 4.27 g

mass of solvent = 15.2 g = 0.0152 kg

[tex]\text{BP}_{solvent} = 100^{\circ}\text{C}[/tex]

[tex]K_{\text{b}} = 0.512^{\circ}\text{C/}m[/tex]

solute: C₁₂H₂₂O₁₁

solvent: H₂O

Required:

boiling point of the solution

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass of solute = (12.01 g/mol × 12) + (1.008 g/mol × 22) + (16.00 g/mol × 11)

molar mass of solute = 342.296 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{4.27 g}}{\text{342.296 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solute = 0.012474583 mol

Step 3: Calculate the molality.

[tex]m = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]m = \frac{\text{0.012474583 mol}}{\text{0.0152 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]m = 0.82069625 \: m[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the boiling point elevation.

[tex]\Delta T_{\text{b}} = K_{\text{b}}m[/tex]

[tex]\Delta T_{\text{b}} = (0.512^{\circ}\text{C/}m)(0.82069625 \: m)[/tex]

[tex]\Delta T_{\text{b}} = 0.42^{\circ}\text{C}[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the boiling point of solution.

[tex]\text{BP}_{solution} = \text{BP}_{solvent} + \Delta T_{\text{b}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{BP}_{solution} = 100^{\circ}\text{C} + 0.42^{\circ}\text{C}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{BP}_{solution} = 100.42^{\circ}\text{C}}[/tex]

Answer:

d. 100.42°C
