17. narrative report in making chocolate moron​


Answer:

Chocolate Moron is a delicious snack filled with both chocolate and marshmallow. It is a classic recipe that has been around for generations and is a favorite among kids and adults alike.

At our school, the students in our advanced cooking class decided to make Chocolate Moron as part of their final project. To start, they gathered all the needed ingredients, which included butter, sugar, cocoa, marshmallows, baking soda, and vanilla extract.

Next, they melted the butter in a large bowl and added the sugar, cocoa, and baking soda. Once mixed together, the mixture was spread out in a greased 9x13 inch pan. After that, the marshmallows were placed on top of the mixture and the pan was placed in the oven for about 30 minutes.

Once the Chocolate Moron was ready, the students removed the pan from the oven and let it cool for about 10 minutes. After that, they cut the Chocolate Moron into squares and topped it with some melted chocolate and marshmallow cream.

The students were very pleased with their final product and enjoyed tasting it. All their hard work paid off and their Chocolate Moron was a delicious success. It was a great experience for the students to learn how to make a classic recipe, and it was a fun way to end their cooking class.

23. How does a high school student help in the preservation of Filipino native delicacies?​


Similar to why one should keep a surname, preserving your culture and tradition as people is a way for you to connect with your racial roots and cultural identity. Your language, your ethnic dressing, your native cooking, your songs and dances, your folklores and myth, your cultural behaviour, your writing, etc. are what draws others in curiosity to find the richness of your culture. Your ‘essence’ as a person is rooted into all these behaviour. Japan will lose part of its essence if you remove the geishas and sumo wrestlers, as an example. India will lose part of its essence if the people stopped wearing their traditional sarees and national costumes, and without the image of its yogis and gurus.

There may be many traditional ways that have become dated and un-useful in modern times, but it should still be recorded, remembered and preserved as they are part of what makes your culture unique and interesting. If you only want to ‘ape’ the ways of other nationalities, you lose your essence as people and there is no longer something worth knowing about your race as it has become a generic way of life that can be found anywhere else in the world.
