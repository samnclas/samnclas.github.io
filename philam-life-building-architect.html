16. 2. Cite some reasons why architects and engineers use triangles when they build structures likebuildings, bridges, etc​


Why do engineers use triangles in construction?

When engineers build structures, they want to make sure that the structure can bear weight. In other words, they do not want the structure to fall down when a force is applied to it. One of the shapes that can bear weight very well is the triangle.

Why are triangles used in architecture?

The two most common triangular forms used in architecture are equilateral and isosceles. Triangles are effective tools for architecture and are used in the design of buildings and other structures as they provide strength and stability. This is why many residential homes have A-frames; it provides a sturdy structure.

Why are triangles used in bridges?

Triangles are used in bridges because they evenly distribute weight without changing their proportions. When force is applied on a shape like a rectangle it would flatten out. Before triangles were used in bridges, they were weak and could not be very big.
