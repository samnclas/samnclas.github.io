3. Which of the following examples below use a correct in-text citation using APA format?a. (Verbo, 2016)b. (Verbo).(167-176)c. (Verbo, 167-176)d. (Verbo) (2016)​


(Verbo, 2016) used a correct in-text citation using APA format.

What is APA Format?

The preferred format for virtually all social scientific research subjects is known as APA Style, or American Psychological Association. Its goal was to make scientific writing more uniform. Citations aid the author in avoiding plagiarism and the grave repercussions that follow it.

Academic materials, including books and scholarly journal articles, should be written and formatted according to the APA style. In the field of behavioral and social sciences, such as sociology, education, health sciences, criminal justice, and anthropology, in addition to psychology, it is frequently used to cite sources.

The American Psychological Association (APA format) is frequently used to cite sources in the social sciences, education, and psychology. The fundamental rules for the APA style were first established in a 1929 article in Psychological Bulletin. The APA Publication Manual eventually expanded on these recommendations.

As you are composing your paper, it's crucial to reference the sources of the material you use in your text. These annotations are known as in-text citations, and according to APA style, you must include the author's name followed by the publication date when citing sources within the body of your article.
