3. The poh of a solution is 10.40 calculate the hydrogen ion concentration in the solution


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the pH of the solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{pH} & = 14 - \text{pOH} \\ & = 14 - 10.40 \\ & = 3.60 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the hydrogen ion concentration.

[tex]\begin{aligned} [\text{H}^+] & = 10^{-\text{pH}} \\ & = 10^{-3.60} \\ & = \boxed{2.5 \times 10^{-4} \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the hydrogen ion concentration in the solution is 2.5 × 10⁻⁴ M.

5. The ionization constant for water (Kw) is 9. 311 × 10−14 at 60 °C. Calculate [H3O+], [OH−], pH, and pOH for pure water at 60 °C.


Answer:

At 60℃, ionization constant of water,

Kw = [H3O^+] x [OH^-] = 9.311×10^-14

As [H3O^+] = [OH^-] in pure water,

[H3O^+] = [OH^-] =√(9.311×10^-14) = 3.05×10^-7 mol/L.

So, pH = -log [H₃O^+] = -log(3.05×10^-7) =6.515

and pOH = -log [OH^-]

= -log(3.05×10^-7) = 6.515

23. Activity 1. 1. Calculate the pH of each of the following solutions: a. A solution has a [H+] of 1 * 10 ^ - 9 * M b. [ H +] of the solution is 1 * 10 ^ - 3 * M2. What is the pH of a solution with a [ H +] of 4.2 * 10 ^ - 10 * M ? (use calculator in solving this problem) 3. The calculated pH of a solution is 6. What is the [ H +] of the solution? 4. Gatorade, a popular energy drink has a [ H +] of 1.0 x 10 M. Calculate its pH and pOH. 5. A commercial apple juice has a [ H +] of 2.5 * 10 ^ - 6 * M What is its pOH?​


HEART & 5 STAR LANG OKS NAKO

Answer:

1. a. pH = -log([H+]) = -log(110^-9) = 9

b. pH = -log([H+]) = -log(110^-3) = 3

2. pH = -log([H+]) = -log(4.2*10^-10) = 9.38

3. pH = 6

-log([H+]) = 6

[H+] = 10^-6 = 1 * 10^-6 M

4. [H+] = 1.0 x 10 M

pH = -log([H+]) = -log(1.0 x 10) = 1

pOH = 14 - pH = 14 - 1 = 13

5. pOH = -log([OH-])

Since [H+][OH-] = 1.0 x 10^-14 at 25°C:

[OH-] = 1.0 x 10^-14 / [H+] = 1.0 x 10^-14 / 2.5 x 10^-6

pOH = -log(4.0 x 10^8) = 8.4
