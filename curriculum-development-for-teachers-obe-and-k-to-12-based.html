7. What adaptations are to be made to access the K to 12 Curriculum based on the needs of the LWDs?​


Answer:

K to 12 refers to the Philippine educational system that includes Kindergarten (K) and 12 years of elementary and secondary education. The curriculum for K to 12 education is designed to provide students with a holistic and well-rounded education that includes both academic and non-academic subjects.

There are various adaptations that can be made to the K to 12 curriculum to better meet the needs of learners with disabilities (LWDs). Some examples of such adaptations may include:

Modifications to the curriculum content: This may involve simplifying or adapting the material to make it more accessible to LWDs, or providing additional resources or support to help LWDs understand and engage with the material.

Use of assistive technology: Assistive technology such as screen readers, speech-to-text software, or adapted keyboards can help LWDs access and engage with digital resources and materials.

Adjustments to the learning environment: This may involve making physical accommodations such as installing ramps or handrails, or providing assistive devices such as wheelchairs or hearing aids. It may also involve providing additional support or resources to help LWDs participate in activities and assignments.

Differentiated instruction: This may involve providing LWDs with individualized or small group instruction to address their specific needs and learning styles.

Overall, the adaptations made to the K to 12 curriculum for LWDs should be based on the specific needs and abilities of each individual learner, and should aim to provide LWDs with equal opportunities and support to succeed in their education.

Length of Education: The old curriculum typically covers a 10-year basic education cycle (e.g., primary and secondary education), while the K to 12 curriculum extends the basic education to a 12-year cycle, typically consisting of Kindergarten, 6 years of primary education, 4 years of junior high school, and 2 years of senior high school.

Focus on Specializations: The K to 12 curriculum includes a Senior High School (SHS) program, which offers specialized tracks or strands that allow students to focus on specific fields, such as STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics), Humanities and Social Sciences, Accountancy and Business Management, and Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) among others.
