9. put in simple words the statement calm before storm


Answer:

"calm before the storm" refers to a period of quiet or calm that occurs before an event or situation that is expected to be tumultuous or chaotic. It can be used to describe a lull in activity or a sense of peacefulness that precedes a period of intense activity or conflict. Essentially, it is a warning that something big or significant is about to happen, and that it is important to stay calm and prepared in order to weather the impending storm.

The eye is a region of mostly calm weather at the center of tropical cyclones. The eye of a storm is a roughly circular area, typically 30–65 kilometers (19–40 miles) in diameter.

27. If the center of the storm is generally calm, why are people afraid to be hit by the eye of the storm?​


Answer:

The center of the storm, which is also called the eye of the storm, is typically the calmest part of the storm. That’s why so many people are drawn to the eye of the storm, thinking that it’s the best place to be during a hurricane. But while the eye of the storm may feel calm and safe, it’s actually the most dangerous place to be. The eye of the storm often contains the highest winds in a hurricane, and these winds are the reason why the eye of the storm is the most dangerous part of the storm.
