21. What is overexertion or overtraining? What are the safety protocols​


Overtraining

When a person exercises more than their body can recuperate from intense activity, overtraining results. When a person is overtrained, their performance may plateau or decline as a result of their inability to maintain a certain level of performance or training load, which exceeds their capacity for recuperation. Overtrained individuals stop improving and may even start to lose fitness and strength. In athletics, overtraining is sometimes referred to as chronic weariness, burnout, and overstress.

Further explanation

Advice on How to Prevent Overtraining Injuries

Avoid escalating the level of workout difficulty too rapidly.

Progression must be gradual. You may prevent injuries by making and adhering to a plan that organizes your workout toward your goals.

Slow down and control your expectations.

In other words, start off slowly. When you start doing intense exercise after being inactive for a while or try to reach your fitness goal rapidly, overtraining injuries can occur quickly.

Be aware of your body.

You can be overdoing it if you have persistent soreness or pain, or if a particular exercise puts strain on your body. Overtraining is an indication of fatigue as well.

Take rest periods.

Not arranging rest days is a typical sign of overtraining. Restorative days are necessary for the continued health of bones, joints, and muscles.

Feed your body nourishing nutrients.

Make sure your diet contains enough proteins, grains, veggies, fruits, and calcium-rich foods.

Rest.

Sleep is crucial to our body's capacity for growth and repair.
