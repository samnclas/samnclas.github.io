Understanding language

The notion of language is a system of human communication expressed through the arrangement of sounds or written expressions that are structured to form larger units, such as morphemes, words, and sentences.

The Role of Language in People's Life

Language includes a symbolic system that is used reciprocally, and is formed from the sound elements of human speech.

Language is also part of the most basic human abilities, as well as being the main feature of the Homo sapiens species.

According to linguists, although animals have the ability to use symbols or signs to communicate, the communication system is not a language.

Different from animals, humans are able to exchange speech through language to interact with each other. So, the main function of language is to meet the needs of communication between human beings.

In addition, in human life, language is also closely related to cultural development. There are 3 factors that show the role of language in cultural development.

First, language as a cultural element. Every activity in human life has an element of language in it. So, to understand the development of a culture, it is necessary to first examine the development of language in society. Second, language as a marker of social stratification. Language can show patterns of relationships and social stratification in a society. Third, language as a symbol of ethnic culture. Language can show cultural symbols in an ethnic group. This is evident from the existence of various dialects or dialects from various ethnic groups. This is due to geographical differences and the stratification of the social environment between ethnic groups in people's lives.

30. This type of Media Code refers to colors, object, body language, clothing and setting - Include the language, dress and actions of characters knows as mise-en-scene. Technical Codes Written Codes Symbolic Codes Audio Codes ​


Symbolic Codes

Symbolic Codes are the type of Media Codes that refers to the colors, object, body language, clothing, and setting. It also includes the .language, dress, and actions of characters known as mise-en-scene.

They are social in nature which means that these codes are actually depicted outside of the media products but can still be understood or related to by the audience.

Setting – answers the question of when and where. It tells about the place and time of the media. Mise en Scene – means ‘everything within the frame'. It refers to the description of an object that is found in a frame and the arrangement. Mise en scene shall include an analysis of the set design, costume, props, staging, and composition. Acting – it deals with the portrayal of the characters. It can be seen in facial expressions, body language, vocal qualities, movement, and body contact. Color – it shows connotations and is highly cultural in nature. Its aspects include the dominance of color, foils that are contrasting, and symbolism.
