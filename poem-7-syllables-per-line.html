A quatrain is a kind of poetry that is formed with a series of four lines. They create one verse of the poem or otherwise known as a stanza. It is also one of the most popular forms of poetry because of how flexible it is and compatible with different rhyme schemes and rhythmic patterns.

8. make a poem with 14 lines and 5-7 syllables​


Answer:

When my love swears that she is made of truth

I do believe her, though I know she lies,

That she might think me some untutor'd youth,

Unlearned in the world's false subtleties.

Thus vainly thinking that she thinks me young,

Although she knows my days are past the best,

Simply I credit her false speaking tongue:

On both sides thus is simple truth suppress'd.

But wherefore says she not she is unjust?

And wherefore say not I that I am old?

O, love's best habit is in seeming trust,

And age in love loves not to have years told:

Therefore I lie with her and she with me,

And in our faults by lies we flatter'd be.

NEW NORMAL

Never committed a crime,

but now I feel like a prisoner.

Trapped in our minds,

our spirits leashed,

our existence wanders among these all too familiar walls.

How have we not become hysterical?

Have we accepted this insanity as the new normal?

For those that thrive from the bells of laughter and the “hustling and bustling,”

now feel trapped, surrounded by radio silence.

Reluctantly and unexpectedly we have signed up to a new “quarantine routine.”

A simple notion of could you imagine Corona coming here?

has now become our reality.

This muffled routine encompasses not only COVID-19,

but ongoing issues of human cruelty, racial injustice,

as well as social and political division more than ever before.

We can’t help but wonder, when will it all end?

“New normal” is repeatedly stressed across the media,

but when will life as we knew it come back?

Do we even want that back anymore?

We continue to navigate through these unsolicited times,

grapple with these uncertainties,

but must bear in mind to find ourselves amongst the chaos.

Despite the incessant isolation,

the turmoil, and the division,

no one is alone.

Be kind to one another and ourselves,

seek and provide help to one another.

Work on yourself,

continue to explore and spread love,

and try to free yourself from the chaos of it all.

30. Assignment:Make a poem about FAMILY. 3 stanzas, 4 lines per stanza, 7 syllables per lines. Have it handwritten on a bondpaperyung maganda sana HA at hindi mali mali ​


Poem about Family (3 stanzas, 4 lines per stanza, 7 syllables per line)

MOM

The shadows beneath her eyes are evidence of the sleepless night

Her agreements were an effort to give you everything right

For your elegance is the wrinkles on her face

After the entire world, she never had her own space

The epiphany of your happiness makes her day bright

The only blessing she has is to have you in her sight

The only reason she is smiling is that you are alive

She always puts you first when no one has left

She has given up her entire life so that you may grow

Do not complain about her age-related slow

The winds are whistling as she sits alone in a chair

She quietly awaits her conclusion and prepares to retire

STANZA

In poetry, a stanza describes the primary structural unit of a poem. Comparable to a song's lyrics or a paragraph in prose, a stanza is a unit of poetry consisting of related lines.

The number of lines, the predominant meter, and the rhyme scheme determine the structure of a stanza (also known as a strophe or stave). For example, a quatrain is a stanza consisting of four lines in iambic pentameter with the rhyme scheme abab.
