14. In regards to polar and nonpolar substances, explain this rule, “like dissolves like” or “like mixes with like”.​


Answer:

The phrase “like dissolves like” or “like mixes with like” is a rule of thumb that states that polar substances will dissolve in other polar substances, and nonpolar substances will dissolve in other nonpolar substances. This is because the molecules of a polar substance have an uneven distribution of charge, which allows them to interact with other molecules with similar charges. Nonpolar molecules, on the other hand, have an even distribution of charge and do not interact as strongly with other molecules.
