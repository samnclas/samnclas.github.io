1. 13. What leadership style is currently practiced by the Philippine governmentbureaucratichistorical​


The bureaucratic leadership style is the form of leadership currently practiced by the Philippine government in which the government follows a certain line of authority rather than having a single leader that rules and decides. It was first introduced by Max Weber as an answer to the prevalent “favoritism” in society during the period of industrialization. Bureaucratic leadership has 6 core tenets: (1) hierarchy of authority, (2) division of labor, (3) formal selection, (4) career orientation, (5) rules and regulations, and (6) impersonality.

Hierarchy of authority. The government has a line of command, with tasks and responsibilities for each level of authority being clearly defined.Division of labor/task specialization. This kind of leadership style values the appropriate placement of a person that should be appropriate and qualified in each position with specific tasks. Formal selection. Values the merit system in which people are promoted, hired and fired based on their performance. Career orientation. Provides an opportunity for the employee to have a long-term career based on their performance. Rules and regulations. The system needs a clearly defined set of guidelines, procedures, and rules to keep the organization's structure intact.Impersonality. Focuses on the performance of the entire organization rather than individual contributions.

These tenets can be seen in the three branches of the Philippine government - the executive, legislative, and judiciary with different positions, institutions, and agencies that have different defined expectations and roles. Under the executive branch, we have the president, vice-president, and the cabinets that consist of the secretaries of the 22 executive departments or agencies. Under the legislative branch, we have the senators and representatives or the congressmen and congresswomen. Under the judiciary, we have the Chief justice, secretary of justice, and judges. For instance, the legislative branch will make laws and it is up to the executive branch to implement them. While the law cannot always stand and explain itself to the public, it is the judiciary branch that will explain it, and give justice, whenever the Filipinos need it.

Aside from Bureaucratic leadership, the Philippine government also practices democratic leadership in which they value the participation of the masses in policy-making.
