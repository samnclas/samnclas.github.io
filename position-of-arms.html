I apologize, bacause I can't send pic but I can describe the positions of the arms and feet in ballet:

1. First position of the arms and feet:

- Stand with heels touching and toes turned out to the side.

- Place arms in front of the body, rounded and slightly lifted, with palms facing each other.

2. Second position of the arms and feet:

- Stand with feet apart, toes turned out to the side.

- Raise arms to the sides, slightly below shoulder level, with palms facing down.

3. Third position of the arms and feet:

- Stand with one foot in front of the other, heel to toe, with the back foot turned out to the side.

- Raise one arm above the head in a curved shape, while the other arm rests on the hip.

4. Fourth position of the arms and feet:

- Stand with one foot in front of the other, with a space of one foot's length between them.

- Raise one arm above the head in a curved shape, while the other arm rests on the hip.

5. Fifth position of the arms and feet:

- Stand with one foot crossed in front of the other, with the heel of the front foot touching the toe of the back foot.

- Raise both arms above the head in a curved shape, with palms facing each other.

7. What is arm position?


Question:

What is arm position?

Answer:

There are two basic positions of the arms in ballet. In one, the dancer keeps the fingers of both arms fully touching to form an oval shape, either almost touching the hips, or at navel level, or raised above the dancer's head. In the other, the arms are extended to the sides with the elbows slightly bent.

Positions of the arms in ballet

There are two basic positions of the arms in ballet. In one, the dancer keeps the fingers of both arms fully touching to form an oval shape, either almost touching the hips, or at navel level, or raised above the dancer's head. In the other, the arms are extended to the sides with the elbows slightly bent.
