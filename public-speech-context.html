8. How do speakers apply both verbal and nonverbal behavior in a public speech context?​


Answer:

LEARNING OBJECTIVES

Use verbal and non-verbal audience interaction to keep your audience engaged and involved with your speech

Explanation:

KEY TAKEAWAYS

Key Points

Public speaking is not just a one-way communication of ideas from speaker to audience; an effective public speaker is one who engages and interacts with his or her audience.

Asking questions of your audience, referencing and calling out members of the audience when appropriate, and even just acknowledging and thanking them for their time are all effective ways of verbally interacting with your audience.

Your non-verbal body- language cues such as facial expression, gesture, posture, and eye contact all add layers of interaction with your audience.

Pay close attention to your non-verbal cues as you speak to ensure that your mannerisms match your message.

Answer:

Directions: In your own words, explain in two to three sentences the different strategies in various speech context.  

Explanation:

Intrapersonal  

- Refers to communication that centers on one person in which he or she is both the sender and receiver. Usually are thoughts of a person or a self-talk.

Interpersonal

- This refers to a communication between and among people that has close relationships with each other or among them. This usually is a deeper level of conversation.

Mass  

- Refers to a communication that uses mediums to spread information or output of communication like radios, newspapers, television, internet, and billboards. It has farther reach than any type of communication.

Public  

- Refers to the communication that is delivered to a group usually to advocate or simply give a message to persuade and inspire.  Usually done during rallies or mass gatherings like political sorties.

Dyad  

- A communication that occurs between two people. Their intimacy level is not important.  

Small Group

- A communication that all participants can freely share ideas in a loose and open discussion like an open forum.

22. How will you improve yourself in delivering a speech in public context? Cite concrete examples to support your answer.​


Answer:

By incorporating several public speaking tips into your daily life, you can steadily improve your skills and become more comfortable giving presentations and speeches. These best practices include watching your body language, studying what works well for talented public speakers, practicing breath control, and preparing well in advance of your presentation.

Watching and learning from exceptional speakers offers one of the best ways to improve your own public speaking skills. To start, search for examples of great public speakers on YouTube.

When you watch these individuals, take note of their body language. You'll find that it tends to remain open and inviting. They use their arms and hands in ways that accentuate their key points. They also avoid unnecessary fidgeting and wisely use the space provided to them to capture the audience's attention.

Pay particular attention to how speakers pace themselves. They tend to take their time and deliver their words intentionally. Additionally, they use pauses to keep the audience engaged and do not rush themselves. They may use humor or anecdotes in appropriate and effective ways.

30. 1. Which of the following is NOT a speech context?A. Intrapersonal CommunicationC. Long Distance CommunicationB. Dyad CommunicationD. Public Communication​


Speech context is the way communications are conducted. Speech context is how people relay messages verbally to one another. The two major speech contexts are intrapersonal communication and interpersonal communication. Learning about speech context is important to understand how communications actually as it is used in our daily lives.

Intrapersonal communication is the communication with oneself as when one talks or thinks to self. Interpersonal communication is the communication of one person to another apart from the speaker. Interpersonal communications include:

Dyadic Communication Small Group Communication Public Communication Mass Communication Organizational Communication Intercultural Communication

Long-distance communication is not a speech context.
