1. pagkakaiba ng imperyong romano at imperyong byzantine​


Answer:

The Roman Empire was the post-Republican period of ancient Rome. As a polity it included large territorial holdings around the Mediterranean Sea in Europe, Northern Africa, and Western Asia ruled by emperors.

The Byzantine Empire, also referred to as the Eastern Roman Empire, or Byzantium, was the continuation of the Roman Empire in its eastern provinces during Late Antiquity and the Middle Ages, when its capital city was Constantinople.


2. Kultura ng Imperyong Byzantine


Answer:

A central feature of Byzantine culture was Orthodox Christianity. Byzantine society was very religious, and it held certain values in high esteem, including a respect for order and traditional hierarchies. Family was at the center of society, and marriage, chastity, and celibacy were celebrated and respected.

15. pagtatag ng imperyong byzantine


Answer:

In 330 CE, Emperor Constantine moved the capital of the Roman Empire to Constantinople, in the eastern part of the empire where the language of the majority of people was Greek. When Rome was re-made the capital in 395, it was only the capital of the western part of the empire. Constantinople remained the capital of the eastern roman empire. Although the western roman empire fell in 476 CE, the eastern roman empire remained alive. Byzantine. Beginning in the sixth century, it sought to re-establish political unity in the Mediterranean regions.

THE BYZANTINE GOVERNMENT

In the Byzantine government, the emperor was the absolute head of the government. At first, he was elected by the senate, the people, the army or all three. Later, it became customary to allow the emperor's son to succeed and then remove he is weak or incompetent. The emperor is considered divine and appointed by god. He lives in a magnificent palace and performs in many ceremonies. In civil government, no one is superior to the emperor because he makes the law and enforces it. his power in the church. he appointed the patriarch of constantinople who stands head of the church. he calls the church meetings and he publishes its decree. In general, he supervises everything that the priests do.

CONTRIBUTIONS OF BYZANTIUM TO CIVILIZATION

Byzantine became famous not only for being an empire but for being the center of learning. Byzantine included the knowledge inherited by the Greeks that prevailed and not the Roman civilization. The contribution of the Byzantine Empire to the world is important. Meanwhile, Europe was engulfed in chaos. and interest in learning declined, Byzantine scholars flourished.

23. ambag ni justinian at constantine sa imperyong byzantine?


Si Emperor Constantine (reg. 306-337 CE) ay pinaniniwalaang nagtatag ng Byzantine Empire sa pamamagitan ng kanyang mga reporma at paglilipat ng kabisera ng Roman Empire sa Constantinople (nagngangalang Byzantium noon). Ipinakilala rin niya ang Christianity sa emperyo at nagtatag ng mga batas at patakaran upang itaguyod ang relihiyon na ito.

Si Emperor Justinian I (reg. 527-565 CE) ay nagsulong ng mga reporma sa Byzantine Empire upang mapalakas ang kanyang kapangyarihan at mapanatili ang pagkakaisa ng kanyang mga nasasakupan. Pinangunahan niya ang pagpapalawak ng teritoryo ng emperyo sa pamamagitan ng pakikipaglaban sa iba't ibang kalaban at pag-aangkin ng mga teritoryo. Isinulong din niya ang pagpapakalat ng Byzantine culture at nasimulan ang konstruksyon ng mga makasaysayang gusali tulad ng Hagia Sophia.

Kaya, ang kanilang mga ambag ay hindi lamang nagbigay ng mga pangunahing pangyayari sa kasaysayan ng Byzantine Empire, ngunit nagpabago rin ng kultura at paniniwala ng mga tao sa nasasakupan nila.
