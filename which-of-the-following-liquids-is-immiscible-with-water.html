13. 2. Which of the following liquids is immiscible with water?a. kerosenec. soy sauceb. liquid soapd. vinegar​


Why can't water and oil be combined?

Water (H20) is a short, fat molecule with a slightly positive charge on the hydrogen (H) end, and a slightly negative charge on the oxygen (O) end (the oxygen atom actually "steals" electrons from the hydrogen atom). and are called polar molecules, the negative pole of each water molecule is attracted to the positive pole of the other water molecule, and as a result, the two are tightly bound by hydrogen bonds.

Because the water molecule is a polar molecule, it means that one of the polar ends is negatively charged and the other is positively charged. If the oil is a non-polar molecule which will mix with other non-polar molecules. That's why water molecules can't mix with oil molecules.

But friends, it turns out that we can actually combine these two molecules... how do you do that?

That is by shaking the two molecules into one place for example a bottle. In the bottle we do the emulsion or the unification process by shaking it.

After we shake the oil and water will come together but not for long and they will separate again.

We can hold them together for a long time with the help of emulsifying ingredients, such as soap that we rub into our hands when they are oily and wash with water, between water and oil they unite.

Soap as an emulsifier is a stabilizer between water and oil from the type of surfactant. It has hydrophobic properties which have the property of repelling water and binding oil.

Then you can also use egg yolks as an emulsifier.

Because the atoms in the fatty acids in the oil share their electrons so well, they (usually) have no charge, or at least not enough to make the whole molecule polar. Given their lack of positive or negative charge, they are not attracted to polar molecules like water.
