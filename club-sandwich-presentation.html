12. Club sandwich is it hot or cold​


Answer

A Clubhouse sandwich or Club sandwich is best served warm. However, it can also be served hot or cold depending on the type filling between the bread.

Explanation

A basic Club sandwich is just like a BLT (Bacon, Lettuce, and Tomato) sandwich but with an additional ingredient of ham, poultry meat like chicken or turkey, and/or fried bacon accompanied with a mayonnaise dressing. It is commonly served into four sliced portions with each piece being held together by cocktail sticks or toothpicks (for those cheaper Club Sandwich).

The Clubhouse sandwich has a lot of versions worldwide. Some Club sandwiches have eggs in it that are cooked either sunny side up or scrambled. Sometimes, hot fillings like roasted beef are added which makes it into a hot sandwich. Other versions use cold cuts as fillings which then makes the Clubhouse a cold sandwich

14. Why is it importance of using food picks on club sandwiches and submarine sandwiches?​


Food picks, also known as toothpicks or sandwich picks, are commonly used on club sandwiches and submarine sandwiches to hold the layers of bread and fillings together. Here are a few reasons why using food picks can be important:

Aesthetics: Club sandwiches and submarine sandwiches are typically layered with multiple ingredients, and without food picks, these layers can easily shift and become messy when being cut or handled. Using food picks can help keep the sandwich intact, making it more visually appealing.

Portion control: Food picks can be used to divide a large sandwich into smaller portions. This can be helpful for sharing or for controlling portion sizes, especially in restaurants or catering events.

Convenience: When a sandwich is cut into smaller pieces, it can be easier to eat with your hands. Food picks make it easy to pick up a smaller portion of the sandwich without getting your hands messy.

Overall, while the use of food picks may not be essential, they can help improve the presentation, portion control, and convenience of certain types of sandwiches.
