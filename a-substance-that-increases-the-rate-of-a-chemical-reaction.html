1. A Answer is a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction.


A catalyst is a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction.

Further explanation

In chemical reactions several factors influence:

1. Temperature 2. Pressure 3. Concentration / substance level 4. Stirring 5 Catalysts 6. Surface area

The catalyst itself is a substance that can accelerate the reaction rate but does not change (does not run out / does not react)

Catalysts can be distinguished based on their phases, namely homogeneous catalysts and heterogeneous catalysts

Homogeneous catalyst if in phase with the catalyst, it is generally a solution such as iron (III) chloride solution, FeCl3

Heterogeneous catalysts if not in phase with substances that are catalyzed, generally in the form of solids such as MnO2 powder

Most catalysts are transition elements and their compounds

Catalyst can accelerate the reaction because it can reduce the activation energy (the minimum energy that the reactants have so the reaction can occur)
