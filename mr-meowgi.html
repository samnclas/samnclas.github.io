19. 2. rexual Reproduction meoisis​


Answer:

Sexual reproduction is a type of reproduction that involves a complex life cycle in which a gamete (such as a sperm or egg cell) with a single set of chromosomes (haploid) combines with another to produce an organism composed of cells with two sets of chromosomes (diploid).[1] Sexual reproduction is the most common life cycle in multicellular eukaryotes, such as animals, fungi and plants. Sexual reproduction does not occur in prokaryotes (organisms without cell nuclei), but they have processes with similar effects such as bacterial conjugation, transformation and transduction, which may have been precursors to sexual reproduction in early eukaryotes.

28. definition of meosis​


A type of cell division that results in four daughter cells each with half the number of chromosomes of the parent cell, as in the production of gametes and plant spores.

Meiosis is the type of cell division that occurs in sex cells or gametes. It produces four daughter cells, all with haploid or half the number of complete set of chromosomes. It is important in the generation of genetic variation among the offspring and future generation of a species. Because of this, meiosis is also called reduction-division kind of cell division.

How does meiosis cause genetic variation? A process called crossing-over happens during Prophase I in Meiosis I. It is the exchange of traits among homologous chromosomes (these are chromosomes that have the same size and have same genes and locus within them) in the cell. The reduction from diploid to haploid number of chromosomes in the cell also occurs in Meiosis I, specifically Anaphase I.
