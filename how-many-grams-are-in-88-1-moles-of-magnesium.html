17. how many moles are in 48g of magnesium


Answer:

There are 1.9749 moles of magnesium in 48 grams of magnesium.

Explanation:

This problem can be solved by conversion. The problem requires us to solve the number moles given the mass of the element. To solve this, we need the molar mass of the element.

Molar mass of an element is the mass (in grams) of one mole of the element. In order to find the molar mass of an element, simply look at its atomic weight or atomic mass on the periodic table.

The element is magnesium (Mg), and based on the periodic table, its atomic mass is 24.305 g/mol.

Given:48 g Mgmolar mass Mg = 24.305 g/molSolution:

(48 g Mg) [tex](\frac{1 mol Mg}{24.305 g Mg} )[/tex] = 1.9749 moles Mg

From the unit analysis, we can see that the unit of mass (grams) will be cancelled out, leaving us with the unit moles.

27. Magnesium reacts with hydrochloric acid (HCl) to produce Magnesium chloride ( MgCl2 ) and hydrogen H2 gas (a) How many moles of H2 can be formed by the reaction 0 2.46 moles of HCl with magnesium .How many grams H2 can be formed by the reaction of 0.568 mole of Mg with sufficient HC Mg (s) + 2HCl (aq) ------> MgCl (aq) + H2 (g)


Answer:

(a) 1.23 moles of H2
(b) 1.144 grams of H2 gas

Explanation:

The balanced chemical equation for the reaction of Magnesium with Hydrochloric acid is:

Mg (s) + 2HCl (aq) → MgCl2 (aq) + H2 (g)

(a) To determine the number of moles of H2 gas formed by the reaction of 2.46 moles of HCl with Magnesium, we need to use the stoichiometric coefficients of the balanced equation. From the balanced equation, we can see that 1 mole of Magnesium reacts with 2 moles of Hydrochloric acid to form 1 mole of Hydrogen gas. Therefore, the number of moles of H2 gas formed is:

2.46 moles HCl × (1 mole H2/2 moles HCl) = 1.23 moles H2

Therefore, 1.23 moles of H2 can be formed.

(b) To determine the mass of H2 gas formed by the reaction of 0.568 moles of Mg with sufficient HCl, we again need to use the stoichiometric coefficients of the balanced equation. From the balanced equation, we can see that 1 mole of Magnesium reacts with 1 mole of Hydrogen gas to form MgCl2. Therefore, the number of moles of H2 gas formed is also 0.568 moles.

Now, we can use the molar mass of Hydrogen to determine the mass of H2 gas formed:

0.568 moles H2 × 2.016 g/mol = 1.144 g H2

Therefore, 1.144 grams of H2 gas can be formed.
