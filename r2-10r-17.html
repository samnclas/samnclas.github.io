4. r²-10r=-17 solving quadratic equations by completing the square


SOLVE THE QUADRATIC EQUATION (COMPLETE THE SQUARE)

The solutions that satisfies the quadratic equation r² - 10r = -17 with completing the square are r = 2√3 + 5 and r = -2√3 + 5.

What is Quadratic Equation?

A quadratic equation is an equation with a variable to the second power as its highest power term.

The Standard Form of a Quadratic Equation:

ax² + bx + c = 0

where:

x represents an unknown (variable)a, b, and c represent known numbers, where a ≠ 0

How could you solve such a quadratic equation?

There are four basic ways to solve the quadratic equations:

to factor the quadratic equationto taking the square rootsto use the quadratic formulato complete the square

Usually, you will not be told which method to use. You will have to make that decision yourself.

How to solve the quadratic equation by completing the square?

Steps to Solving Equations by Completing the Square

Rewrite the equation in the form x² + bx = c.Add to both sides the term needed to complete the square.Factor the perfect square trinomial.Solve the resulting equation by using the square root property.

We will solve the given equation with complete square method.

Here are the steps:

The given equation

r² - 10r = -17

Take half of the coefficient of r, square it, then add that to both sides

Half of -10 is -5, and (-5)² is 25, so add 25 to both sidesr² - 10r + 25 = -17 + 25r² - 10r + 25 = 8

Factor the left side

(r - 5)² = 8

Take the square root of both sides

√(r - 5)² = √8 r - 5 = ± √8r - 5 = ± 2√3

Therefore, the solution that satisfies r² - 10r = -17 are

r - 5 = 2√3 ⇔ r = 2√3 + 5 r - 5 = 2√3 ⇔ r = -2√3 + 5
