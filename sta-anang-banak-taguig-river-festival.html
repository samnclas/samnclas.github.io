21. essay for manggahan festival sta. cruz​


Answer:

The Manggahan Festival in Sta. Cruz, Laguna is a celebration of the town's abundance in mangoes, which are known to be one of the best varieties in the Philippines. This festival is a time when the people of Sta. Cruz come together to not only celebrate the town's agricultural heritage but also showcase their culture and traditions.

The festival is held every year during the summer season, usually in late April or early May. The week-long festivities include a variety of events, such as street dancing competitions, beauty pageants, cultural shows, sports tournaments, and food fairs. The highlight of the festival is the grand parade, where colorful floats adorned with mangoes and other fruits parade along the streets.

During the Manggahan Festival, visitors can taste the different varieties of mangoes the town has to offer. Mangoes are also used in various dishes, such as desserts, smoothies, and even main courses. The festival is a perfect opportunity for tourists to experience Sta. Cruz's culinary culture.

Apart from showcasing their mangoes, the festival also highlights the rich history and traditions of Sta. Cruz. The town's cultural shows feature traditional dance performances and music that reflects the town's diverse cultural heritage. Visitors can also witness the traditional practices of the locals, such as the "Pahiyas ng Pagkakamay," which is a ritual that honors the town's patron saint, St. Isidore the Farmer.

The Manggahan Festival is not only a time for celebration, but it also provides opportunities for local businesses to showcase their products and services. Vendors sell various merchandise, from Mango-themed souvenirs to local handicrafts. The festival also attracts tourists to explore the town's landmarks and other tourist attractions.

In conclusion, the Manggahan Festival in Sta. Cruz, Laguna, is a remarkable celebration of the town's agricultural heritage, cultural traditions, and local businesses. It provides a platform for the town to showcase its unique culture and attract tourists to explore its beauty. The festival is a testament to the town's pride in its mangoes, and it's a testament to the town's vibrant community.
