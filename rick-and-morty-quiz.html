25. what is rigor mortis?


Rigor mortis (Latin: rigor "stiffness", mortis "of death") or postmortem rigidity, the third stage of death, is one of the recognizable signs of death, caused by chemical changes in the muscles post mortem, which cause the limbs of the corpse to stiffen. In humans, rigor mortis can occur as soon as 4 hours post mortem.

29. Morty and Rick are discussing whether the number of hours of sleep the night before has an effect on the performance of students for an exam. Write a hypothesis for this situation.


Hypothesis

The hypothesis refers to an educated guess or a possible explanation to the question or problem or situation. It gives a temporary answer to a question. In doing an investigation or research, making a hypothesis is an important part. The hypothesis tells us what investigation to do. This hypothesis can be accepeted or rejected after conducting an experiment or investigation.  The results of the investigation or experiment will dictate whether the hypothesis made should be rejected or accepted.

There are many ways to state a hypothesis. But in research or science investigation, the null and alternative hypothesis is commonly used. The null hypothesis is a type of hypothesis wherein you state in a manner that there one variable has no effect on the other variable. For instance, in the situation give, we can formulate the null hypothesis with this: "The number of hours of sleep the night before has no effect on the performance of students for an exam. "

Another type of hypothesis is the alternative hypothesis. This hypothesis is stated this time in a manner that one variable has an effect on the other variable. Thus, the alternative to the given situation will be: "The number of hours of sleep has an effect on the performance of students for an exam. "

There are many other ways to state a hypothesis. Another way to state the hypothesis is by the if-then hypothesis. Like in the example provided, the hypothesis can be: "If a person has longer hours of sleep the night before the exam, then he/she will perform better in the exam. It can also be the other way around, "If a person has shorter hours of sleep the night before the exam, then he/she will perform poorly in the exam. "

Whatever form of hypothesis we formulate, it is important that we consider that the variables are present and the hypothesis shows the relationship of variables, specifically, the independent and dependent variables. In the situation given, the independent variable is the number of hours of sleep a person has the night before the exam while the dependent variable is the performance of the person in the exam.

Additionally, the hypothesis should lead us also to investigate further. Thus, in making a hypothesis, it should clearly define the role of the variables. A hypothesis should be clearly stated and specific. It should also be well-aligned and coherent to the problem or question. It is capable of being tested through investigation or experiment.
