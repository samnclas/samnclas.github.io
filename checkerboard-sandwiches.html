25. How many squares of all sizes are there on a standard 8-square by 8-square checkerboard?


It's 64 in total.

Solution:

rows = 8

column = 8

quadratic expression =

[tex]8^{2}[/tex] or 8 x 8 = 64

So the total square in the checkerboard games is 64 squares.

Further Info about Quadratic Square

The term "quadratic equation" refers to any polynomial equation that has a degree that is equal to two. The word "quad" refers to four, but the word "quadratic" implies "to make square."

The conventional form of a quadratic equation is written as follows: [tex]ax^{2} + bx +c = 0[/tex]where a, b, and c are all real numbers,[tex]a\neq 0[/tex] is not equal to zero, and [tex]x[/tex] is the variable that is being manipulated.

Certain quadratic expressions can be factored out as perfect squares using the appropriate techniques. For example, [tex]x^{2} +6x+9=(x+3)^{2}[/tex]. However, adding a constant integer to an expression allows us to transform it into an ideal square, even if the expression itself is not one. For instance, [tex]x^{2} +6x+5[/tex] does not constitute a perfect square, but if we add 4, we obtain [tex]x+3^{2}[/tex] as the answer.

The formula for the perfect square is expressed as a combination of two terms, such as [tex](a+b)^{2}[/tex]. The extension of the formula for the ideal square is written as [tex](a+b)^{2}[/tex], which equals [tex]a^{2} +2ab+b^{2}[/tex].
