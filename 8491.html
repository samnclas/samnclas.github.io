3. explain section 31 in RA No. 8491


Answer:

REPUBLIC ACT NO. 8491

AN ACT PRESCRIBING THE CODE OF THE NATIONAL FLAG, ANTHEM, MOTTO, COAT-OF-ARMS AND OTHER HERALDIC ITEMS AND DEVICES OF THE PHILIPPINES.

Be it enacted by the Senate and Home of Representatives of the Philippines in Congress assembled:

SECTION 1. Short Title — This act shall be known as the “Flag and Heraldic Code of the Philippines.”

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy – Reverence and respect shall at all times be accorded the flag, the anthem and other national symbols which embody the national ideals and traditions and which express the principles of sovereignty and national solidarity. The heraldic items and devices shall seek to manifest the national virtues and to inculcate in the minds and hearts of our people a just pride in their native land, fitting respect and affection for the national flag and anthem, and the proper use of the national motto, coat-of-arms and other heraldic items and devices.

13. explanation of flag and heraldic code of the philippines (R.A. 8491)​


Flag and Heraldic Code of the Philippines (R.A. 8491)​

A. DESIGN:

It shall ONLY containing the following: colors are red, blue, and white with a golden-yellow sun that has 8 rays, and three five-pointed stars, as recognized by its citizens.

B. How it shall be displayed and hoisted.

In public buildings, official residence, plazas, and schools/learning institutions all year. In selected places, it shall be hoisted day and night, provided that at night, it is properly illuminated. That on every national holiday, the flag must be hoisted in front of private buildings and residence. It may be displayed whole year round, but a proper flag ceremony must be observed, according to the rules and regulations by the President.all government offices, as well as government-owned corporations are encouraged to observe flag days.merchant ships shall contain the Philippine FlagsOn ground, the flag pole containing the national Flag shall be a prominent place. If attached to a building, it shall be on the roof or anchored upwards. If on stage, or platforms, it shall be on the left side (facing the stage).If the Philippine flag is hoisted alongside another flags, it shall be hoisted on separate poles of the same height, and that the flag shall be of the same size. The Philippine flag shall be hoisted first, and lowered last. In parades, if joined with non-national flag, it shall be in front of the first flank.If signs of being worn-out shows, replace it immediately. A worn-out flag shall not be thrown out. It shall be burned to avoid misuse. The flag shall raised at sunrise or beginning of office/school hours, and lowered at sunset or end of office/school hours. It shall be raised briskly, and lowered ceremoniusly.The flag cannot tough anything below it, like the ground, and flood.
