1. Example of owiwi vocal music of cordillera


BIRTH TO CHILDHOOD MUSIC

 Owiwi - lullabye of Kalinga that relate a child’s life Dagdagay - song of Kalinga that foretells the baby’s future

DEATH RITE MUSIC

Didiyaw - song to a dead child of Bontoc Sangsangit - a dirge of Isneg

WORK OR OCCUPATIONAL SONGS

Sowe-ey - rice pounding song of Bontoc Dakuyon - Kalinga song for hunting bats Dinaweg- Ilongot song for catching wild boar

CEREMONIAL MUSIC

 Kapya - song of Bontoc for curing ceremonies Dawak - song of Ilongot for curing ceremonies

ENTERTAINMENT SONGS

 Hudhud - epic song of Ifugao, a leader chorus style Alim - leader chorus style of music of Ifugao

Vocal Music in Coldillera

1. Ibaloi Badiw- style of singign without prior preparation or practice in leader-chorus form.

2. Owiwi (Kalinga)- a kind of lullaby

3. Dagdagay (Kalinga)- infancy related songs

4. Oppia (Kalinga)- infancy related songs

5. Langan Bata-bata (Tausug)- infancy related songs

6. Bua (Subannen)- infancy related songs

7. Kawayanna- infancy related songs

8. Hudhud- epic poetry that is recited and chanted during occasions.

Music of Mindoro

A rhythmic poetic expression with a meter of seven syllable lines and having rhythmic end-syllables.

It is most often presented as a chant without a determined musical pitch or accompaniment by musical instruments.

                            Palawan

Folk Music of Palawan. Vocal Music : Bagit, Kulial, (song), Tultul (epic chant), Ulit (shamanic chant) Instrumental music : Aruding ( Jew's harp), babarak (ring flute) suling (banded Flute), basal (gong), kusyapiq (lute) , pagang (bamboo zither)u.
