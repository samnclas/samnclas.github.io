17. 1. I would say to the House, as I said to those who have joined the Government, "I have nothing to offer but blood, toil, tears, and sweat. Human values Explanation


Answer:

Winston Churchill speech blood toil tears and sweat

I would say to the House as I said to those who have joined this government: "I have nothing to offer but blood, toil, tears and sweat". We have before us an ordeal of the most grievous kind. We have before us many, many long months of struggle and of suffering.

Explanation:

this phrase refers to any type of work that is strenuous and time-consuming. “I put my blood, toil, tears and sweat into that project,” might be said, meaning the speaker put everything he had into completing the work. It is originally a war phrase that spoke of the painful and difficult job of being a soldier.
