                   Three Law's of Motion

First law of motion " The law of Inertia"

An object at rest will remain at rest unless acted upon by a net force. Net force is an unbalanced force which causes the object at rest to move. An object in motion will remain in motion unless acted upon by a net force. Net force or unbalanced force causes the object to stop moving.

Inertia can be applied in applying force to make the object move and applying force to make the object stop. This can be done every time we do some movement. This can also be done in any aspect of transportation.

Second Law of motion "The Law of Force and Acceleration"

Force causes the object to accelerate. The formula for Force is ( Force = mass × acceleration ). This means that the Force and acceleration when mass is constant is directly proportional. The stronger the force the higher the acceleration. When force is constant, acceleration and mass are inversely proportional. The higher the mass the lesser the acceleration and vise versa.

Third Law of Motion "The Law of action-reaction"

For every action, there should be an equal and opposite reaction. Example: when we walk, we pushed our feet on the ground and the opposite will happened in doing this, and that is we move forward. The same with jumping, you pushed your legs to the ground, and the was opposite, you move upward.
